Article 258 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 addresses the governance of areas within Pakistan that are not part of any province. This article grants the President the authority to make provisions for the peace and good governance of these territories through an order, until such time as Parliament enacts specific laws for these regions. The significance of this article lies in its provision for the administration of territories that do not fall under the jurisdiction of provincial governments, ensuring that all parts of the country are governed effectively and in accordance with the Constitution.

The article has remained unchanged since its inclusion in the original 1973 Constitution, reflecting its foundational role in the governance framework of Pakistan. By empowering the President to act in these territories, the article ensures that there is a mechanism for governance and administration in areas that might otherwise lack structured oversight. This is crucial for maintaining national unity and ensuring that all citizens, regardless of their location within Pakistan, are subject to a consistent legal and administrative framework. The article underscores the flexibility of the constitutional system to adapt to the diverse administrative needs of the country.
