Article 28 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 is dedicated to the preservation of the diverse linguistic, scriptural, and cultural heritage of the country's citizens. It grants the right to any group of citizens with a distinct language, script, or culture to preserve and promote their unique identity. This article is significant as it acknowledges and supports the multicultural and multilingual fabric of Pakistan, ensuring that minority groups have the constitutional backing to maintain their cultural heritage. This provision is crucial in a country like Pakistan, where numerous ethnic groups coexist, each with its own language and cultural practices. By allowing these groups to establish institutions for the preservation and promotion of their cultural identity, the article fosters an environment of inclusivity and respect for diversity.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 28 has not been amended. This consistency indicates a sustained commitment to cultural preservation and the recognition of diversity as a strength of the nation. The article operates within the framework of Article 251, which deals with the national language, ensuring that the promotion of individual languages and cultures does not conflict with the national linguistic policy. The practical effect of this article is the empowerment of cultural groups to maintain their traditions and languages, contributing to the rich tapestry of Pakistan's national identity. By safeguarding these rights, the article helps prevent the marginalization of minority cultures and promotes harmony among the various communities within the country.
