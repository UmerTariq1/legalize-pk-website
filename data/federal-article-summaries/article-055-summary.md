Article 55 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 is concerned with the procedures for voting and maintaining a quorum in the National Assembly. This article establishes the rule that decisions in the National Assembly are made by a majority of the members who are present and voting. However, it specifies that the person presiding over the session does not have a vote unless there is a tie, in which case they cast the deciding vote. This ensures that the presiding officer remains impartial during regular voting but can resolve deadlocks when necessary.

Additionally, Article 55 addresses the issue of quorum, which is the minimum number of members required to be present for the Assembly to conduct its business. It states that if it is brought to the presiding officer's attention that less than one-fourth of the total membership is present during a session, the presiding officer must either adjourn the session or suspend it until the quorum is met. This provision is crucial for maintaining the legitimacy and functionality of the Assembly's proceedings, ensuring that decisions are made with adequate representation.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 55 has not been amended, indicating that its provisions have remained relevant and effective in governing the procedural aspects of the National Assembly. The article plays a vital role in the democratic process by ensuring that decisions are made with a fair representation of elected members and that the Assembly operates efficiently with a sufficient number of participants.
