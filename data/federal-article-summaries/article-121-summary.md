Article 121 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, outlines the types of expenditures that are automatically charged to the Provincial Consolidated Fund. This article is crucial because it specifies the financial obligations that must be met by the provincial government without requiring further legislative approval. The expenditures include the remuneration for key provincial officials such as the Governor, Judges of the High Court, and the Speaker and Deputy Speaker of the Provincial Assembly. It also covers the administrative expenses of the High Court and the Provincial Assembly Secretariat, debt-related charges, and any sums required to satisfy legal judgments against the province.

The purpose of this article is to ensure the financial independence and smooth functioning of essential provincial institutions by guaranteeing that their operational costs are met without delay. This is important for maintaining the rule of law and the effective administration of justice, as well as for the stability of the provincial government. By securing these expenditures, the article helps prevent any potential financial manipulation or political interference that could arise if such expenses were subject to regular budgetary processes.

Since its inclusion in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 121 has not been amended. This stability suggests a consensus on the importance of the financial autonomy of provincial institutions. It reflects the foundational principle that certain expenditures are so critical to the functioning of the provincial government that they should be insulated from the uncertainties of the annual budgetary process. This provision, therefore, plays a key role in upholding the integrity and independence of provincial governance in Pakistan.
