Article 198 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, originally established the principal seats of the High Courts in Pakistan. It ensured that each High Court would continue to operate from the location it occupied before the Constitution came into effect. This provision was crucial for maintaining judicial continuity and stability, ensuring that the High Courts could continue their functions without disruption or relocation, which could have caused administrative and logistical challenges.

The Eighteenth Amendment, enacted in 2010, significantly expanded Article 198. It introduced several new clauses that addressed the geographical distribution of judicial authority more comprehensively. A notable addition was the establishment of a High Court for the Islamabad Capital Territory, with its principal seat in Islamabad. This change recognized the administrative significance of Islamabad as the capital and ensured that it had its own High Court jurisdiction. Additionally, the amendment mandated the creation of benches in various cities across Pakistan, including Bahawalpur, Multan, Rawalpindi, Peshawar, Dera Ismail Khan, Abbottabad, Mingora, Bannu, Quetta, Sibi, and Turbat. These changes aimed to make the judicial system more accessible to citizens by reducing the distance they needed to travel to seek legal redress.

The amendment also provided for the appointment of judges to these benches, with the Chief Justice of the High Court responsible for appointments, subject to the President's approval. Furthermore, it allowed for the creation of rules governing the practice and procedure of each bench, formulated by the Governor in consultation with the Chief Justice. This decentralization of judicial authority was intended to enhance the efficiency and responsiveness of the legal system, making it more attuned to regional needs and reducing the burden on the principal seats of the High Courts. The changes reflected a broader effort to democratize access to justice and ensure that the judiciary could effectively serve the diverse and widespread population of Pakistan.
