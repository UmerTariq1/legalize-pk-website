Article 36 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, is dedicated to the protection of minorities. This article is a crucial component of the constitutional framework as it underscores the commitment of the State to safeguard the rights and interests of minority groups within the country. The article mandates that minorities should have their legitimate rights protected, which includes ensuring their due representation in both Federal and Provincial services. This provision is significant as it aims to promote inclusivity and equality, ensuring that minority communities have a voice and presence in governmental structures, which is essential for fostering a diverse and harmonious society.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 36 has not been amended. This consistency indicates a sustained commitment to the principles of minority protection as initially envisioned by the framers of the Constitution. The lack of amendments suggests that the foundational intent and language of the article have remained relevant and adequate in addressing the needs of minority communities in Pakistan. By maintaining this article unchanged, the Constitution continues to affirm the importance of minority rights as a fundamental aspect of Pakistan's democratic and pluralistic ethos.
