Article 194 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 pertains to the oath of office for the Chief Justice and other judges of the High Courts. This article establishes the formal procedure that must be followed before these judicial officers can assume their duties. Specifically, it requires the Chief Justice of a High Court to take an oath before the Governor, while other judges take their oath before the Chief Justice. The oath is a solemn promise to uphold the Constitution and perform their duties with integrity, and it is set out in the Third Schedule of the Constitution. This process is crucial as it underscores the commitment of the judiciary to the rule of law and the principles enshrined in the Constitution, ensuring that judges are bound by a formal declaration of their responsibilities.

The article was amended by the Eighteenth Amendment in 2010, which introduced a specific provision for the Islamabad High Court. This amendment required the Chief Justice of the Islamabad High Court to take their oath before the President of Pakistan, while other judges of this court would take their oath before the Chief Justice of the Islamabad High Court. The establishment of the Islamabad High Court was a significant development, reflecting the administrative and judicial needs of the federal capital. By specifying the oath-taking process for this court, the amendment ensured consistency and clarity in the judicial appointment process across different regions of Pakistan. This change was part of broader constitutional reforms aimed at enhancing the independence and efficiency of the judiciary, and it highlighted the evolving nature of Pakistan's judicial system in response to the country's administrative requirements.
