Article 120 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, is concerned with the financial governance of the provinces. It mandates that each Provincial Government must present an Annual Budget Statement to the Provincial Assembly for every financial year. This statement is a detailed account of the estimated receipts and expenditures of the Provincial Government. The purpose of this article is to ensure transparency and accountability in the financial operations of the provincial governments, allowing the Provincial Assembly to scrutinize and approve the proposed budget. This process is crucial as it ensures that public funds are allocated and spent in a manner that aligns with the priorities and needs of the province, and it provides a mechanism for the elected representatives to oversee and influence financial decisions.

The article specifies that the Annual Budget Statement must separately show two types of expenditures: those that are charged upon the Provincial Consolidated Fund by the Constitution, and other expenditures proposed to be made from the same fund. Additionally, it requires a distinction between expenditure on revenue account and other types of expenditure. This distinction is important because it helps in understanding how much of the budget is being spent on day-to-day operations versus long-term investments or capital projects. By laying out these requirements, Article 120 plays a vital role in the financial management and legislative oversight within the provincial framework of Pakistan, ensuring that there is a structured and transparent approach to budgeting at the provincial level. 

Since its inception in 1973, Article 120 has not been amended, indicating that its original provisions have been deemed sufficient to meet the needs of provincial financial governance. This stability suggests that the framework established by the article has been effective in its role, providing a consistent process for budgetary oversight and accountability in the provinces.
