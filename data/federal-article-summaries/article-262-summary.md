Article 262 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 establishes the use of the Gregorian calendar for all constitutional purposes. This article ensures that any reference to time periods within the constitutional framework is standardized according to the internationally recognized Gregorian calendar, which is the calendar system most widely used around the world. The adoption of this calendar is significant as it provides a uniform method for calculating dates and time periods, which is crucial for legal clarity and consistency in the application of constitutional provisions. This standardization helps avoid confusion that could arise from using multiple calendar systems, especially in a diverse country like Pakistan where different cultural and religious calendars might be in use.

Since its inclusion in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 262 has not been amended. The lack of amendments suggests that the use of the Gregorian calendar has remained uncontroversial and effective for the purposes it serves. By maintaining a consistent time-reckoning system, the article supports the smooth functioning of governmental and legal processes, ensuring that all constitutional timelines, such as those for elections, legislative sessions, and other governmental functions, are calculated in a uniform manner. This consistency is vital for the predictability and stability of the constitutional order in Pakistan.
