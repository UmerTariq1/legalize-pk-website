Article 178 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 pertains to the oath of office for the Chief Justice and other judges of the Supreme Court. This article establishes the formal procedure that must be followed before these high-ranking judicial officials can assume their duties. Specifically, it requires the Chief Justice of Pakistan to take an oath before the President, while other judges of the Supreme Court must take their oath before the Chief Justice. The oath is a solemn promise to uphold the Constitution and perform their duties with integrity, as detailed in the Third Schedule of the Constitution. This process is crucial as it underscores the commitment of the judiciary to the rule of law and the principles enshrined in the Constitution, thereby reinforcing public trust in the judicial system.

The article was amended once through the Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025. The amendment made a subtle yet significant change by specifying that the oath is to be taken by the "Chief Justice of Supreme Court" rather than the "Chief Justice of Pakistan." While this might seem like a minor linguistic adjustment, it reflects a broader trend towards standardizing terminology and possibly aligning the nomenclature with other jurisdictions. The practical effect of this change is largely symbolic, as it does not alter the oath-taking process or the duties of the Chief Justice. However, it may have been motivated by political or administrative considerations to ensure clarity and consistency in the constitutional language.
