Article 80 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 is concerned with the presentation of the Annual Budget Statement by the Federal Government to the National Assembly. This article establishes the procedural framework for how the government must present its financial plans for the upcoming year. It mandates that the government provide a detailed account of its expected income and expenditures, which is crucial for maintaining transparency and accountability in the management of public funds. The Annual Budget Statement is a key document that outlines the government's financial priorities and strategies, and it serves as a basis for legislative scrutiny and approval.

The article specifies that the budget must clearly differentiate between two types of expenditures: those that are charged upon the Federal Consolidated Fund by the Constitution, and other expenditures proposed to be made from this fund. This distinction is important because expenditures charged upon the Federal Consolidated Fund are typically obligatory and include items such as debt repayments and salaries of certain constitutional offices, which cannot be altered by the legislature. By requiring this separation, the article ensures that the National Assembly can effectively oversee and debate discretionary spending, thus playing a critical role in the democratic process.

Since its inclusion in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 80 has not been amended. This stability suggests that the procedures and principles it establishes have been deemed effective and appropriate for the financial governance of Pakistan. The article's enduring presence underscores its foundational role in the country's budgetary process, ensuring that the government remains accountable to the elected representatives and, by extension, to the citizens of Pakistan.
