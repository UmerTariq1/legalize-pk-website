Article 5 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, establishes the fundamental duties of loyalty to the state and obedience to the Constitution and the law. This article underscores the foundational principle that every citizen's primary duty is to remain loyal to Pakistan. Additionally, it mandates that all citizens, regardless of their location, as well as any individuals present within Pakistan, must adhere to the Constitution and the laws of the country. This provision is crucial as it sets the expectation for civic responsibility and legal compliance, forming the bedrock of the relationship between the state and its citizens. By emphasizing loyalty and obedience, the article seeks to ensure national unity and the rule of law, which are essential for the stability and governance of the country.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 5 has not been amended, indicating its enduring significance and the consensus on its necessity in the constitutional framework. The lack of amendments suggests that the principles enshrined in this article have been consistently upheld as fundamental to the nation's identity and governance. By establishing these duties, the article plays a critical role in fostering a sense of national identity and legal order, which are vital for the functioning of a democratic state. The article's unaltered status reflects its acceptance and the importance placed on these duties by successive governments and the populace.
