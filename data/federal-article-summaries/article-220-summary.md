Article 220 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, establishes a crucial obligation for all executive authorities within the Federation and the Provinces. It mandates that these authorities must assist the Commissioner and the Election Commission in performing their duties. This article is significant because it ensures that the Election Commission, which is responsible for overseeing free and fair elections, receives the necessary support from the executive branch of the government. By requiring cooperation from all levels of executive authorities, the article aims to uphold the integrity and efficiency of the electoral process, which is foundational to a functioning democracy.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 220 has remained unchanged, reflecting its fundamental role in the constitutional framework of Pakistan. The lack of amendments suggests a consensus on the importance of maintaining a robust support system for the Election Commission, ensuring that it can operate independently and effectively. This provision helps to prevent any undue influence or obstruction from executive authorities, thereby safeguarding the democratic process and reinforcing the principle of free and fair elections. The enduring nature of this article underscores its critical importance in maintaining the balance of power and ensuring that the electoral process is conducted smoothly and transparently.
