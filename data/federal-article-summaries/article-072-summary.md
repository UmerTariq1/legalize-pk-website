Article 72 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 outlines the procedures for joint sittings of the two Houses of Parliament: the National Assembly and the Senate. This article is crucial as it provides a framework for how these joint sessions are to be conducted, ensuring that there is a clear and organized method for legislative processes that require the involvement of both Houses. The President, in consultation with the Speaker of the National Assembly and the Chairman of the Senate, is empowered to establish rules for these joint sittings. This includes determining who will preside over the meeting, typically the Speaker of the National Assembly, unless otherwise specified by the rules.

The article also stipulates that any rules made regarding the procedure of joint sittings must be presented at such a sitting and can be modified or replaced during the session. Decisions made during these joint sittings are determined by a majority vote of the members present and voting, which underscores the democratic principle of majority rule in legislative decision-making. This article is significant as it ensures that there is a structured approach to resolving legislative matters that require the consensus of both Houses, thereby facilitating cooperation and coordination within the bicameral legislative system of Pakistan.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 72 has not been amended. This indicates that the procedures it established have been deemed effective and sufficient for managing joint sittings of Parliament. The stability of this article suggests that the mechanisms it provides have successfully supported the legislative process in Pakistan, allowing for efficient communication and decision-making between the National Assembly and the Senate.
