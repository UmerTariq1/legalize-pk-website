Article 274 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 addresses the vesting of property, assets, rights, liabilities, and obligations between the Federal Government and the Provincial Governments. This article is crucial as it delineates the distribution of governmental responsibilities and resources following the establishment of the Constitution. It ensures a clear transition of control over assets and liabilities that were previously managed by either the Federal or Provincial Governments, depending on the new constitutional responsibilities assigned to each level of government. This is significant in maintaining administrative order and preventing disputes over resource allocation and responsibility, which are essential for the smooth functioning of the federal structure of Pakistan.

The article specifies that any property or assets vested in the President or the Federal Government before the Constitution's commencement would continue to be vested in the Federal Government unless they were used for purposes that became provincial responsibilities. Conversely, assets vested in a Provincial Government would remain so unless they were used for federal purposes. Additionally, it addresses the continuity of rights, liabilities, and obligations, ensuring that these remain with the respective government unless there is a constitutional shift in responsibility. This provision is vital for legal and financial continuity, ensuring that contracts and obligations are honored and that there is no disruption in governance due to the transition.

Since its inception, Article 274 has not been amended, indicating that its original provisions have remained relevant and effective in managing the distribution of governmental responsibilities and resources. Its unaltered state suggests that the framework it established has been sufficient to address the needs of both the Federal and Provincial Governments in terms of property and liability management, reflecting its foundational role in the constitutional structure of Pakistan.
