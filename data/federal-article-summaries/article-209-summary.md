Article 209 of the Constitution of Pakistan establishes the Supreme Judicial Council, a body responsible for overseeing the conduct and capacity of judges in the higher judiciary, including the Supreme Court and High Courts. Its primary purpose is to ensure judicial accountability and maintain the integrity of the judiciary by investigating allegations of misconduct or incapacity among judges. This article is crucial in the constitutional framework as it provides a mechanism to uphold the rule of law and ensure that judges adhere to a code of conduct, thereby maintaining public confidence in the judicial system.

The article has undergone several amendments, reflecting evolving perspectives on judicial accountability. The First Amendment in 1974 clarified the seniority criteria for Chief Justices of High Courts, excluding acting Chief Justices from consideration, which aimed to bring more clarity and fairness in determining the composition of the Council. The Eighteenth Amendment in 2010 expanded the Council's authority, allowing it to initiate inquiries on its own, rather than solely on the President's direction, thereby enhancing its independence and proactive role in judicial oversight.

Significant changes came with the Twenty-sixth and Twenty-seventh Amendments in 2024 and 2025, respectively. These amendments restructured the Council to include the Chief Justice of a newly established Federal Constitutional Court, reflecting a broader approach to judicial oversight. The amendments also introduced provisions for addressing inefficiency in addition to misconduct and incapacity, and mandated the establishment of a secretariat to support the Council's functions. These changes aimed to strengthen the Council's capacity to maintain judicial standards and adapt to the complexities of modern governance. The introduction of a Federal Constitutional Court and its inclusion in the Council's composition suggests a political and structural shift towards a more comprehensive judicial oversight mechanism, likely in response to demands for greater judicial accountability and efficiency.
