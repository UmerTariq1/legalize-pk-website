Article 4 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, establishes the fundamental right of individuals to be treated in accordance with the law. This article is significant because it enshrines the principle of legal protection and due process, which are cornerstones of a fair and just legal system. It asserts that every citizen and every person within Pakistan's jurisdiction has the inalienable right to enjoy the protection of the law. This means that individuals cannot be arbitrarily deprived of their life, liberty, body, reputation, or property without legal justification. Furthermore, it ensures that individuals are free to engage in activities that are not prohibited by law and are not compelled to perform actions that the law does not mandate.

The importance of Article 4 lies in its role as a safeguard against arbitrary actions by the state or other entities. By guaranteeing that actions affecting individuals must be in accordance with the law, it provides a legal framework that protects citizens from abuse of power. This article is foundational in maintaining the rule of law in Pakistan, ensuring that all actions taken by the government or individuals are subject to legal scrutiny. It is a critical component of the constitutional structure as it underpins the legal rights of individuals and reinforces the accountability of authorities.

Since its inclusion in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 4 has not been amended. This stability indicates a consistent commitment to the principles of legal protection and due process. The unchanging nature of this article reflects its fundamental importance in the constitutional framework and its role in upholding the rights of individuals in Pakistan. Its presence in the Constitution serves as a constant reminder of the state's obligation to respect and protect the legal rights of all individuals within its jurisdiction.
