Article 182 of the Constitution of Pakistan deals with the appointment of ad hoc judges to the Supreme Court. This provision is crucial for ensuring the smooth functioning of the judiciary, particularly when there is a shortage of judges or when the existing judges are unable to form a quorum to conduct court proceedings. The article allows the Chief Justice of Pakistan to temporarily appoint additional judges to the Supreme Court, thereby maintaining the court's ability to handle its caseload efficiently. This mechanism is vital for upholding the rule of law and ensuring that justice is delivered without undue delay.

The original text of Article 182, as introduced in the 1973 Constitution, empowered the Chief Justice of Pakistan to appoint ad hoc judges with the President's approval. The first significant change came with the Nineteenth Amendment in 2011, which introduced a requirement for the Chief Justice to consult with the Judicial Commission before making such appointments. This amendment aimed to enhance transparency and accountability in the judicial appointment process by involving a broader range of stakeholders. The Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025 further refined the language by specifying the role of the "Chief Justice of Supreme Court," likely to clarify the position and responsibilities within the judicial hierarchy. These amendments reflect an ongoing effort to balance judicial independence with the need for oversight and collaboration in judicial appointments.
