Article 106 of the Constitution of Pakistan establishes the framework for the composition and election of Provincial Assemblies in the country. It outlines the number of general seats and seats reserved for women and non-Muslims in each provincial assembly, ensuring representation across different demographics. This article is crucial as it provides the structural basis for provincial governance, allowing citizens to elect their representatives who will legislate on provincial matters. The inclusion of reserved seats for women and non-Muslims underscores Pakistan's commitment to inclusive representation, acknowledging the diverse fabric of its society.

Over the years, Article 106 has undergone several amendments, reflecting the evolving political and social landscape of Pakistan. The Second Amendment in 1974 introduced specific provisions for the representation of the Ahmadi community, highlighting the political and religious dynamics of the time. The Fourth Amendment in 1975 expanded the reserved seats for non-Muslims, indicating a move towards broader inclusivity. The Eighth Amendment in 1985 extended the reservation of seats for women, demonstrating a continued effort to enhance gender representation in provincial legislatures.

The most significant changes came with the Eighteenth Amendment in 2010, which redefined the composition of the assemblies, specifying the number of general and reserved seats for each province and introducing a proportional representation system for reserved seats. This amendment aimed to strengthen provincial autonomy and ensure fair representation of political parties. The Twenty-fifth Amendment in 2018 further adjusted the seat allocation for Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, incorporating the Federally Administered Tribal Areas into the provincial framework, a move that was part of a broader effort to integrate these regions into the national political system. These amendments collectively reflect Pakistan's ongoing efforts to adapt its constitutional framework to better represent its diverse population and address regional disparities.
