Article 175-I, introduced through the Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025, establishes the retirement age and term limits for the Chief Justice and other judges of the Federal Constitutional Court in Pakistan. This article specifies that judges of this court will serve until they reach the age of sixty-eight, unless they choose to resign earlier or are removed according to constitutional procedures. The Chief Justice, however, has a distinct term limit of three years, after which they must retire, regardless of whether they have reached the age of sixty-eight. This provision ensures a regular turnover in the leadership of the court, potentially allowing for fresh perspectives and preventing the concentration of power.

The introduction of this article reflects a significant development in the judicial structure of Pakistan, emphasizing the importance of both stability and renewal in the judiciary. By setting a fixed term for the Chief Justice, the amendment aims to balance the need for experienced leadership with the benefits of periodic change. This change could have been motivated by political considerations, such as the desire to prevent any single individual from holding too much influence over an extended period, thereby promoting judicial independence and accountability. The amendment's practical effect is to ensure that the highest positions within the Federal Constitutional Court are subject to regular review and change, which can enhance the court's adaptability and responsiveness to evolving legal and social contexts.
