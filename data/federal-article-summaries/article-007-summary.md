Article 7 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, provides a clear definition of what constitutes "the State" within the legal framework of the country. This article is foundational as it delineates the entities that are considered part of the State, which includes the Federal Government, Parliament, Provincial Governments, Provincial Assemblies, and any local or other authorities in Pakistan that have the legal power to impose taxes or cesses. This definition is crucial because it establishes the scope of governmental authority and responsibility, ensuring that all these bodies are recognized as part of the State's structure. It is important for maintaining clarity in governance and ensuring that the powers and duties of these entities are understood within the context of the Constitution.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 7 has not been amended, indicating that the original definition of the State has remained relevant and sufficient for the governance needs of Pakistan. The lack of amendments suggests that the foundational understanding of what constitutes the State has been stable and uncontested, reflecting a consensus on the roles and responsibilities of these governmental bodies. This stability is significant as it provides a consistent framework for interpreting the powers and limitations of various state entities, which is essential for the rule of law and the functioning of a democratic society.
