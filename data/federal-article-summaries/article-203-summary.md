Article 203 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 establishes the supervisory role of High Courts over subordinate courts. This article is crucial as it ensures a hierarchical judicial structure where High Courts have the authority to oversee and manage the functioning of lower courts within their jurisdiction. The purpose of this supervision is to maintain consistency, efficiency, and fairness in the judicial process, ensuring that justice is administered effectively across all levels of the judiciary. By empowering High Courts with this supervisory role, the Constitution aims to uphold the rule of law and protect the rights of citizens by ensuring that lower courts adhere to legal standards and procedures.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 203 has remained unchanged, indicating a consistent recognition of the importance of judicial oversight by High Courts. The lack of amendments suggests that the foundational principle of judicial supervision has been deemed effective and necessary for maintaining the integrity of the judicial system in Pakistan. This unaltered status reflects a consensus on the essential role of High Courts in providing guidance and oversight to ensure that subordinate courts function properly and justly, thereby reinforcing public confidence in the legal system.
