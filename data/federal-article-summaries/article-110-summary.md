Article 110 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 establishes the right of the Governor of a province to address the Provincial Assembly. This article is significant as it provides the Governor, who is the ceremonial head of the province, with a formal mechanism to communicate directly with the elected representatives of the province. By allowing the Governor to address the assembly, the article ensures that the executive branch, represented by the Governor, can convey important messages, policy directions, or concerns to the legislative body. This interaction is crucial for maintaining a coherent relationship between the executive and legislative branches at the provincial level, facilitating governance and ensuring that the assembly is informed of the executive's perspective.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 110 has not been amended. This stability suggests that the provision has been deemed effective and uncontroversial in fulfilling its purpose. The article's unaltered state reflects a consensus on the importance of maintaining a channel for the Governor to address the assembly, which can be vital during times of political or administrative challenges. By requiring the attendance of assembly members for such addresses, the article underscores the importance of these communications and ensures that they are given due attention by the provincial legislators.
