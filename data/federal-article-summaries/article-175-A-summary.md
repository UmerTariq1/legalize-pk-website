Article 175-A of the Constitution of Pakistan establishes the process for appointing judges to the Supreme Court, High Courts, Federal Shariat Court, and, following recent amendments, the newly created Federal Constitutional Court. This article is crucial as it outlines the composition and functioning of the Judicial Commission of Pakistan, which is responsible for nominating judges, and the Parliamentary Committee, which confirms these nominations. The article aims to ensure a transparent and balanced process involving both judicial and political elements, reflecting the importance of maintaining judicial independence while incorporating democratic oversight.

Initially introduced by the Eighteenth Amendment in 2010, Article 175-A has undergone several amendments that have significantly altered its structure and scope. The Nineteenth Amendment in 2011 expanded the membership of the Judicial Commission and adjusted the nomination process to enhance transparency and accountability. The Twenty-sixth Amendment in 2024 introduced performance evaluations for High Court judges, adding a layer of accountability and ensuring that judges maintain high standards of efficiency. This amendment also restructured the Commission to include more diverse representation, such as a woman or non-Muslim member, reflecting a broader commitment to inclusivity.

The most recent Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025 further expanded the scope of Article 175-A by including the Federal Constitutional Court in the judicial appointment process. This amendment redefined the roles within the Judicial Commission and Parliamentary Committee, emphasizing the importance of proportional representation and seniority in judicial appointments. It also introduced a mechanism for appointing the Chief Justices of the Federal Constitutional Court and the Supreme Court, ensuring a systematic and fair approach. These changes reflect ongoing efforts to adapt the judicial appointment process to the evolving political and social landscape of Pakistan, aiming to strengthen the judiciary's independence and integrity.
