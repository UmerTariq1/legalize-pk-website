Article 103 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 outlines the conditions under which a Governor can hold office. This article is crucial as it establishes the framework for the impartiality and independence of the Governor, who is a key representative of the federal government in each province. The article ensures that the Governor does not have conflicting interests by prohibiting them from holding any other office of profit or any position that offers remuneration for services. This is intended to prevent any potential conflicts of interest that could arise if the Governor were to have financial ties to other roles within the government or private sector.

Furthermore, Article 103 stipulates that a Governor cannot simultaneously be a member of Parliament or a Provincial Assembly. If a sitting member of these legislative bodies is appointed as Governor, they must vacate their legislative seat upon assuming the governorship. This provision is significant as it maintains the separation of powers and ensures that the Governor remains a neutral figure, not influenced by legislative responsibilities or political affiliations. By preventing the Governor from holding legislative office, the article aims to uphold the integrity and impartiality of the Governor's role as an overseer of provincial administration and a representative of the President of Pakistan.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 103 has not been amended, indicating a consistent approach to maintaining the independence and neutrality of the Governor's office. This stability reflects the importance placed on the role of the Governor as a non-partisan figure who can effectively mediate between the federal and provincial governments, ensuring that the constitutional framework operates smoothly across Pakistan's diverse political landscape.
