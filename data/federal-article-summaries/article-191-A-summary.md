Article 191-A of the Constitution of Pakistan was introduced through the Twenty-sixth Amendment in 2024. This article established the concept of Constitutional Benches within the Supreme Court. These benches were to be composed of judges nominated by the Judicial Commission of Pakistan, ensuring representation from each province. The purpose of these benches was to handle specific jurisdictions of the Supreme Court, including original, appellate, and advisory jurisdictions, particularly in cases involving constitutional questions or substantial legal interpretations. This structure aimed to streamline the handling of significant constitutional matters and ensure that such cases were addressed by a specialized group of judges.

However, this article underwent a significant change with the Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025, which resulted in the omission of the entire text of Article 191-A. The removal of this article suggests a shift in the approach to handling constitutional matters within the Supreme Court, potentially reverting to the previous system where such cases were not exclusively handled by designated Constitutional Benches. The omission could have been driven by political considerations or a reassessment of the judicial process, reflecting the dynamic nature of constitutional law and governance in Pakistan. This change would have affected how constitutional cases were managed, possibly impacting the efficiency and specialization of judicial proceedings in the Supreme Court.
