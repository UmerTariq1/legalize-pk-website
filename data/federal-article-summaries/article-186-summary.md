Article 186 of the Constitution of Pakistan, originally established in 1973, pertains to the advisory jurisdiction of the Supreme Court. This article allows the President of Pakistan to seek the Supreme Court's opinion on any legal question deemed of public importance. The purpose of this provision is to enable the President to obtain legal clarity on complex issues, ensuring that decisions made at the highest level of government are informed by the judiciary's interpretation of the law. This mechanism serves as a bridge between the executive and judicial branches, promoting a harmonious relationship and ensuring that the executive actions align with constitutional principles.

In 2025, the Twenty-seventh Amendment brought a significant change to Article 186 by omitting its content entirely. This amendment, enacted during Asif Ali Zardari's tenure, effectively removed the President's ability to refer questions of law to the Supreme Court for advisory opinions. The practical effect of this change is a reduction in the formal interaction between the executive and judiciary on matters of legal interpretation, potentially limiting the President's access to judicial guidance on issues of public importance. The political motivations behind this amendment could be speculated upon, but the specific reasons for its enactment are not detailed in the provided text. Nonetheless, this change marks a shift in the constitutional framework, altering the dynamics of how legal advice is sought and provided at the highest levels of government.
