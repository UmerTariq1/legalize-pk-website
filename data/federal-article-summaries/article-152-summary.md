Article 152 of the Constitution of Pakistan initially established the framework for the acquisition of land by the federal government for purposes connected with matters over which Parliament has the authority to legislate. This provision allowed the Federation to require a province to acquire land on its behalf or to transfer provincial land to the Federation, with terms agreed upon by both parties or determined by an arbitrator appointed by the Chief Justice of Pakistan. This article was significant as it facilitated the federal government's ability to implement projects of national importance that required land, ensuring that federal initiatives could proceed even if they involved provincial territories.

The article underwent significant changes through constitutional amendments. The Seventeenth Amendment in 2003, under President Pervez Musharraf, omitted the title of the article, although the content remained unchanged. This amendment was part of a broader set of changes that aimed to consolidate executive powers and streamline governance, reflecting the political climate of the time which prioritized federal authority. Later, the Eighteenth Amendment in 2010, under President Asif Ali Zardari, completely omitted the content of Article 152. This amendment was part of a comprehensive effort to decentralize power, enhance provincial autonomy, and rectify the balance of power between the federal and provincial governments. By removing the article, the amendment underscored a shift towards empowering provinces and reducing federal overreach, aligning with the broader objectives of the Eighteenth Amendment to strengthen the federal structure of Pakistan.
