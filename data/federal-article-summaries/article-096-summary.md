Article 96 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, establishes the procedure for a vote of no-confidence against the Prime Minister. This article is crucial as it provides a mechanism for the National Assembly to express its lack of confidence in the Prime Minister, thereby ensuring that the head of government retains the support of the majority of the elected representatives. The article outlines a structured process that requires not only the proposal of a no-confidence motion but also the nomination of a successor within the same resolution. This ensures that there is always a proposed alternative ready to take over, maintaining political stability and continuity in governance.

The article also includes specific procedural rules to prevent the misuse of the no-confidence motion. For instance, it cannot be moved during the consideration of the annual budget, and there are time constraints on when the vote can occur after the motion is introduced. Furthermore, if a no-confidence motion fails, a new motion cannot be introduced for another six months, preventing constant challenges to the Prime Minister's authority. An interesting provision is the disregard of votes from members of a political party who vote against the party line in support of a no-confidence motion, provided the majority of their party opposes it. This clause, effective for ten years from the constitution's commencement or until the second general election, whichever is later, was likely intended to maintain party discipline and prevent political instability during the nascent stages of Pakistan's democratic development.

Overall, Article 96 plays a vital role in balancing the power between the executive and the legislature, ensuring that the Prime Minister remains accountable to the National Assembly while also safeguarding against frivolous or destabilizing attempts to unseat the government. Its inclusion reflects the framers' intent to create a robust parliamentary system where the executive is directly answerable to the elected representatives of the people.
