Article 158 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the priority of natural gas requirements within the country. It establishes that the province where a natural gas well-head is located has the first right to meet its own needs from that well-head before the gas is distributed to other parts of Pakistan. This provision is significant because it recognizes the importance of natural resources to the provinces where they are found, allowing them to benefit directly from their local resources. This can be seen as a measure to ensure that the provinces have the necessary resources to support their own development and energy needs, which is crucial for regional economic stability and growth.

The article has remained unchanged since it was first introduced in the original 1973 Constitution under the leadership of Prime Minister Zulfikar Ali Bhutto. This stability suggests a continued consensus on the importance of granting provinces priority access to their natural resources. By ensuring that provinces can first satisfy their own energy requirements, the article helps to balance regional interests and promote equitable resource distribution across the country. This provision is particularly important in a federal structure like Pakistan's, where resource allocation can be a contentious issue between the central government and provincial authorities.
