Article 202 of the Constitution of Pakistan, established in the original 1973 document, grants High Courts the authority to create rules governing their own procedures as well as those of subordinate courts. This article is crucial because it empowers the judiciary to manage its internal processes, ensuring that courts operate efficiently and consistently. By allowing High Courts to regulate their procedures, the article supports judicial independence, a fundamental principle in a democratic society. It ensures that the judiciary can function without undue interference from the legislative or executive branches, maintaining a balance of power within the government.

Since its inception, Article 202 has not been amended, indicating that its original provisions have remained relevant and effective in the context of Pakistan's legal framework. The stability of this article suggests that the mechanism it establishes for procedural regulation has been sufficient to meet the needs of the judiciary over the years. The ability of High Courts to tailor their procedures to their specific requirements helps in addressing the unique challenges they face, thereby enhancing the overall efficiency and responsiveness of the judicial system.

In practical terms, this article impacts citizens by potentially speeding up the judicial process and making it more transparent. When courts have the autonomy to set their procedures, they can implement changes that reduce delays and improve access to justice. For the judiciary, it provides the flexibility to adapt to new legal challenges and technological advancements, ensuring that the court system remains modern and effective. Overall, Article 202 plays a vital role in maintaining the integrity and functionality of Pakistan's judicial system.
