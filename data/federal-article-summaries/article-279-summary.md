Article 279 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 is concerned with the continuation of taxes. It ensures that all taxes and fees that were in place under any law before the Constitution came into effect on August 14, 1973, would continue to be collected. This provision was crucial for maintaining fiscal stability and continuity in government operations during the transition to the new constitutional framework. By allowing existing taxes to remain in effect until they are changed or removed by the appropriate legislative body, the article provided a seamless transition in the financial and administrative operations of the state.

The article has not been amended since its inception, indicating its foundational role in the fiscal policy of Pakistan. Its unaltered state suggests that the mechanism it established for the continuation of taxes has been effective and uncontroversial, providing a stable basis for government revenue collection. This stability is essential for the government to fund public services and infrastructure, which are critical for the country's development and the welfare of its citizens. The article underscores the importance of legislative oversight in tax matters, ensuring that any changes to the tax system are made through a formal legislative process, thereby upholding democratic principles and accountability.
