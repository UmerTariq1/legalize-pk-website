Article 9-A of the Constitution of Pakistan, introduced through the Twenty-sixth Amendment in October 2024, establishes the right of every person to a clean, healthy, and sustainable environment. This addition reflects a growing recognition of environmental issues and their impact on public health and quality of life. By enshrining this right in the Constitution, Pakistan acknowledges the importance of environmental protection as a fundamental human right, aligning with global trends and international environmental standards.

The introduction of this article signifies a significant shift in the constitutional framework, emphasizing the state's responsibility to ensure environmental sustainability. This amendment was enacted during the tenure of Asif Ali Zardari, suggesting a political commitment to addressing environmental concerns, which may have been driven by increasing public awareness and demand for action on climate change and pollution. The practical effect of this change is to empower citizens to hold the government accountable for environmental degradation, potentially leading to more robust environmental policies and enforcement mechanisms. This constitutional guarantee could also influence legislative and judicial actions, encouraging the development of laws and decisions that prioritize environmental health and sustainability.
