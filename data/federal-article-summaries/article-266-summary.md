Article 266 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 serves a crucial transitional purpose by officially repealing the Interim Constitution of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan. This article marks a significant moment in Pakistan's constitutional history as it signifies the transition from the interim legal framework to a permanent constitutional structure. The Interim Constitution was a temporary arrangement that guided the country following the dissolution of earlier constitutional frameworks. By repealing it, Article 266 underscores the establishment of a more stable and enduring constitutional order under the 1973 Constitution, which was intended to provide a comprehensive and lasting legal foundation for the governance of Pakistan.

The importance of Article 266 lies in its role in solidifying the legal and political transition to the 1973 Constitution, which was crafted to reflect the democratic aspirations of the people of Pakistan. This transition was essential for the consolidation of democratic governance and the rule of law in the country. The repeal of the Interim Constitution through Article 266 effectively nullified any previous amendments, modifications, or orders that were part of the interim legal framework, thereby ensuring that the new Constitution would be the supreme law of the land without any remnants of the temporary provisions that preceded it.

Since Article 266 has never been amended, its original purpose and function remain intact. It continues to symbolize the definitive shift from provisional governance to a more permanent and structured constitutional regime. This article is a testament to the commitment of the framers of the 1973 Constitution to establish a stable and enduring legal system that would guide Pakistan's political and legal institutions in the years to come.
