Article 222 of the Constitution of Pakistan deals with the framework for electoral laws. It grants Parliament the authority to legislate on various aspects of elections, including the allocation of seats in the National Assembly, the delimitation of constituencies, the preparation of electoral rolls, and the conduct of elections. This article is crucial as it lays down the legal foundation for organizing free and fair elections, which are a cornerstone of democratic governance. It ensures that elections are conducted in an orderly manner and that any disputes or corrupt practices are addressed through legal mechanisms. Importantly, it also safeguards the powers of the Election Commission, ensuring that any laws made by Parliament do not undermine the Commission's authority.

The article was originally part of the 1973 Constitution and remained unchanged until the Twenty-second Amendment in 2016. This amendment expanded the scope of Article 222 by including the delimitation of constituencies for local governments, in addition to those for the National Assembly and Provincial Assemblies. It also extended the provision for the due constitution of local governments. This change was significant as it recognized the importance of local governments in the democratic process and aimed to enhance their representation and governance. The amendment reflected a broader political commitment to decentralization and empowering local governance structures, which can lead to more responsive and accountable administration at the grassroots level.
