Article 163 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the power of Provincial Assemblies to impose taxes on individuals engaged in various professions, trades, callings, or employments. This article is significant because it delineates the fiscal autonomy of provincial governments within the federal structure of Pakistan. By allowing provinces to levy taxes on professional activities, the article empowers them to generate revenue independently, which is crucial for addressing local needs and priorities. However, it also sets a boundary by specifying that such taxes should not be considered as taxes on income, which typically fall under the jurisdiction of the federal government. This distinction helps maintain a balance between provincial autonomy and federal oversight in fiscal matters.

Since its inclusion in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 163 has not been amended. This stability suggests that the provision has effectively served its purpose without necessitating changes. The article's unaltered state reflects a continued consensus on the importance of allowing provinces the ability to raise funds through professional taxes while ensuring that income taxation remains a federal prerogative. This balance is crucial for maintaining the financial equilibrium between different levels of government in Pakistan, ensuring that both provincial and federal authorities can function effectively within their respective domains.
