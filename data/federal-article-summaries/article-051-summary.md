Article 51 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 outlines the composition and electoral framework of the National Assembly, which is the lower house of the Parliament of Pakistan. This article is crucial as it establishes the democratic foundation for the representation of the people in the legislative process. It specifies the number of seats, the criteria for voter eligibility, and the allocation of seats among the provinces, the Federally Administered Tribal Areas (FATA), and the Federal Capital. The article also provides for reserved seats for women and non-Muslims, ensuring inclusivity and representation of diverse groups in the legislative process.

Since its inception, Article 51 has undergone several amendments reflecting the evolving political and demographic landscape of Pakistan. The Fourth Amendment in 1975 introduced additional seats for certain groups, while the Eighth Amendment in 1985 extended the reservation of seats for women. The most significant changes came with the Eighteenth Amendment in 2010, which increased the total number of seats to 342, including specific allocations for women and non-Muslims, and introduced a proportional representation system for reserved seats. This amendment aimed to enhance representation and inclusivity in the National Assembly.

Further adjustments were made with the Twenty-fourth and Twenty-fifth Amendments in 2017 and 2018, respectively. These amendments revised the seat allocation based on the latest census data and addressed the integration of FATA into Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, reducing the total number of seats to 336. These changes were politically significant as they responded to demographic shifts and administrative reforms, particularly the merger of FATA, which was a major political development. Overall, the amendments to Article 51 reflect Pakistan's efforts to adapt its legislative framework to changing political realities and ensure fair representation of its diverse population.
