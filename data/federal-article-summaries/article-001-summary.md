Article 1 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 defines the territorial composition of the country and establishes Pakistan as a Federal Republic known as the Islamic Republic of Pakistan. This article is foundational as it outlines the geographical boundaries and political structure of the nation, which is crucial for governance, administration, and the application of laws. It specifies the provinces and territories that make up Pakistan, ensuring clarity on the jurisdiction of the Constitution and the federal nature of the state.

The article has undergone several amendments reflecting the political and administrative changes in the country. The First Amendment in 1974 removed references to East Pakistan following its secession and the creation of Bangladesh in 1971. This amendment also streamlined the language regarding the territories of Pakistan. The Eighteenth Amendment in 2010 updated the names of the provinces to reflect current nomenclature, changing "Baluchistan" to "Balochistan," "North-West Frontier" to "Khyber Pakhtunkhwa," and "Sind" to "Sindh." These changes were part of broader efforts to address provincial autonomy and identity.

The most significant change came with the Twenty-fifth Amendment in 2018, which merged the Federally Administered Tribal Areas (FATA) with Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. This amendment was a major step towards integrating these areas into the mainstream political and administrative framework of Pakistan, aiming to provide equal rights and development opportunities to the residents of these regions. Each amendment to Article 1 reflects the evolving political landscape of Pakistan and the ongoing efforts to address regional identities and governance challenges.
