Article 161 of the Constitution of Pakistan addresses the financial distribution of revenues generated from natural resources, specifically natural gas and hydro-electric power. Initially introduced in the 1973 Constitution, this article ensures that the financial benefits derived from these resources are allocated to the provinces where the resources are located. This is significant because it acknowledges the rights of provinces to benefit directly from the natural resources found within their territories, promoting a sense of ownership and encouraging local development. By stipulating that the net proceeds from federal duties and royalties on natural gas, as well as profits from hydro-electric power generation, should be paid to the respective provinces, the article aims to balance the financial interests between the federal and provincial governments.

The Eighteenth Amendment in 2010 brought a notable change to Article 161 by expanding its scope to include oil, in addition to natural gas. This amendment added a provision that the net proceeds of the federal duty of excise on oil, similar to natural gas, should also be paid to the province where the oil well-head is situated. This change was part of a broader effort to decentralize power and enhance provincial autonomy, reflecting the political climate at the time which favored greater provincial control over local resources. The practical effect of this amendment was to increase the financial resources available to provinces with oil reserves, thereby potentially boosting their economic development and enabling them to invest more in local infrastructure and services. This shift was significant in the context of Pakistan's federal structure, as it aimed to address longstanding provincial grievances regarding resource distribution and fiscal autonomy.
