Article 107 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 establishes the duration of a Provincial Assembly, specifying that it will continue for a term of five years from the date of its first meeting unless it is dissolved earlier. This article is crucial as it sets the temporal framework for the functioning of provincial legislatures, ensuring a regular cycle of democratic governance and accountability at the provincial level. By defining a fixed term, the article provides stability and predictability in provincial governance, allowing elected representatives to plan and implement policies over a set period.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 107 has not been amended. This stability suggests a consensus on the importance of maintaining a consistent term length for Provincial Assemblies, reflecting the foundational principle of periodic elections as a cornerstone of democratic governance. The lack of amendments also indicates that the five-year term has been deemed appropriate for balancing the need for continuity in governance with the democratic imperative of allowing citizens to periodically express their will through elections. This article plays a vital role in ensuring that provincial governments remain accountable to the electorate while providing them with sufficient time to execute their legislative agendas.
