Article 271 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 initially established the framework for the formation and functioning of the first National Assembly under the new constitutional order. This article was crucial as it provided continuity from the previous legislative body to the newly established National Assembly, ensuring a smooth transition during a pivotal moment in Pakistan's political history. It specified that the first National Assembly would consist of members who had already taken the oath under the previous assembly, and it set the term of this assembly to last until August 14, 1977, unless dissolved earlier. The article also addressed the qualifications and disqualifications for membership, aligning them with the Interim Constitution of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan, and included provisions for filling casual vacancies.

The Fourth Amendment in 1975 introduced significant changes to Article 271. It expanded the composition of the first National Assembly to include not only those who had taken the oath before the commencement of the new Constitution but also those elected to fill additional seats as per Article 51. This amendment was likely a response to the evolving political landscape and the need to accommodate a broader representation within the legislative framework. By including these additional members, the amendment aimed to enhance the inclusivity and representativeness of the National Assembly. The amendment also made a minor adjustment by referencing Article 63, which deals with disqualifications for membership, thus ensuring that the provisions were consistent with the broader constitutional framework.

Overall, Article 271 and its subsequent amendment played a vital role in shaping the early legislative structure of Pakistan under the 1973 Constitution. It facilitated a seamless transition from the old to the new political order while adapting to the changing political dynamics through the Fourth Amendment. This ensured that the National Assembly could function effectively and representatively during a critical period in Pakistan's democratic development.
