Article 102 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 establishes the requirement for the Governor of a province to take an oath of office before assuming their duties. This oath must be administered by the Chief Justice of the High Court of the respective province. The specific wording of the oath is detailed in the Third Schedule of the Constitution. The purpose of this article is to ensure that the Governor, who is a key figure in the provincial government, formally commits to upholding the Constitution and performing their duties with integrity. This process is crucial as it underscores the legal and ethical responsibilities of the Governor, reinforcing the rule of law and the constitutional framework within which provincial governance operates.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 102 has not been amended. This stability suggests that the process of administering the oath to the Governor has been deemed effective and uncontroversial, requiring no changes over the years. The unaltered nature of this article reflects a consensus on the importance of the oath-taking ceremony as a foundational step in the assumption of gubernatorial responsibilities, ensuring that the Governor is publicly committed to their constitutional obligations. This article plays a vital role in maintaining the integrity and accountability of provincial leadership in Pakistan.
