Article 42 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 is concerned with the oath-taking process of the President of Pakistan. This article establishes the requirement for the President to take an oath before assuming office, ensuring that the individual pledges to uphold the Constitution and perform their duties faithfully. The oath is a formal declaration of commitment to the constitutional framework and the responsibilities of the presidency. It is a crucial step in the transition of presidential power, symbolizing the legal and ethical obligations of the officeholder to the state and its citizens.

Originally, the article specified that the President must take the oath before the Chief Justice of Pakistan. This requirement underscores the importance of the judiciary in the constitutional process, as the Chief Justice represents the highest legal authority in the country. However, the Twenty-seventh Amendment, enacted on November 13, 2025, introduced a significant change by altering the official before whom the oath is taken. The amendment replaced the Chief Justice of Pakistan with the Chief Justice of the Federal Constitutional Court. This change reflects a shift in the institutional structure, possibly indicating a reorganization or enhancement of the constitutional court system, which may have been driven by political or judicial reforms aimed at strengthening constitutional oversight. The practical effect of this amendment is a reallocation of ceremonial and constitutional responsibilities, potentially impacting the dynamics between different branches of government.
