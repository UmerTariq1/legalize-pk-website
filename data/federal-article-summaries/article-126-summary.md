Article 126 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the financial powers of a provincial government when the Provincial Assembly is dissolved. This provision is crucial because it ensures the continuity of government operations and financial stability even when the legislative body is not in session. Specifically, it allows the provincial government to authorize expenditures from the Provincial Consolidated Fund for up to four months. This temporary measure is in place until the normal procedure for approving government spending, as outlined in Articles 122 and 123, can be completed once the Assembly is reconstituted. This mechanism is vital for maintaining government functions and services without interruption, which is particularly important in times of political transition or instability.

The article has remained unchanged since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, indicating its foundational role in the financial governance framework of Pakistan's provinces. By providing a clear process for managing provincial finances during periods when the Assembly is not operational, Article 126 helps prevent potential financial crises and ensures that essential services continue to be funded. This stability is crucial for both the government and the citizens, as it prevents disruptions that could affect public services and economic activities. The lack of amendments suggests that the provision has effectively served its purpose without necessitating revisions, reflecting its importance and functionality within the constitutional structure.
