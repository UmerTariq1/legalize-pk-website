Article 50 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 establishes the Parliament of Pakistan, which is a bicameral legislature consisting of two houses: the National Assembly and the Senate. This article is foundational in defining the legislative structure of Pakistan, setting the stage for how laws are made and how the government is held accountable. The National Assembly is typically responsible for representing the population, with members elected directly by the people, while the Senate represents the provinces, ensuring that regional interests are considered in the legislative process. This dual-house system is crucial for balancing the diverse interests of Pakistan's population and regions, promoting a more inclusive and representative form of governance.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 50 has not been amended, indicating that the basic structure of Pakistan's Parliament as a bicameral body has remained consistent. This stability suggests a continued commitment to a legislative framework that balances direct representation with regional interests. The unamended status of this article reflects its foundational role in the constitutional framework, providing a stable basis for legislative processes and governance in Pakistan. The existence of both the National Assembly and the Senate allows for a comprehensive approach to law-making, where both the populous and the provinces have a voice, thereby reinforcing the federal nature of the state.
