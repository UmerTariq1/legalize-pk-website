Article 173 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, outlines the powers of the executive authority at both the federal and provincial levels concerning property and contractual matters. This article is crucial as it establishes the framework within which the government can acquire, manage, and dispose of property, as well as enter into contracts. It ensures that the executive branch has the necessary authority to manage resources effectively, which is essential for governance and the implementation of public policies. The article also delineates the legal protections for the President and Governors, ensuring that they are not personally liable for contracts made in their official capacities, thus safeguarding the continuity and stability of executive functions.

The article has remained unchanged since its inception in 1973, indicating that its provisions have been deemed adequate and effective in addressing the needs of the government concerning property and contracts. The original text provides a comprehensive structure for the acquisition and management of property by the government, ensuring that such actions are conducted under the authority of the appropriate legislative acts. By vesting property acquired for governmental purposes in the respective governments, it maintains clear ownership and accountability. Additionally, the requirement that contracts be made in the name of the President or Governor, and the stipulation that they are not personally liable, underscores the separation of personal and official responsibilities, which is a fundamental principle in public administration.

The absence of amendments to this article suggests that it has successfully fulfilled its intended purpose without necessitating changes. Its role in the constitutional framework is significant as it supports the operational capabilities of the government, allowing it to function effectively in managing public resources and entering into necessary agreements for the welfare of the state and its citizens.
