Article 207 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 establishes rules regarding the post-retirement conduct of judges from the Supreme Court and High Courts. Its primary purpose is to ensure the independence and impartiality of the judiciary by preventing conflicts of interest that could arise if judges were to hold other profitable positions immediately after their tenure. Specifically, it prohibits judges from holding any other office of profit in the service of Pakistan if it increases their remuneration or involves remuneration for services rendered. Additionally, it restricts former judges from taking up certain positions for two years after leaving office, except for specific judicial or quasi-judicial roles, or roles like the Chief Election Commissioner or positions within the Council of Islamic Ideology.

The article was amended by the Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025, which introduced the Federal Constitutional Court into the provisions. This amendment expanded the scope of the article to include judges from this new court, reflecting a structural change in Pakistan's judiciary. The amendment also removed specific references to the High Court of West Pakistan, which was a historical reference that had become obsolete. By including the Federal Constitutional Court, the amendment ensured that judges from this court are subject to the same post-retirement restrictions as those from the Supreme Court and High Courts, thereby maintaining the integrity and independence of the judiciary across all levels. This change likely reflects a political and legal evolution in Pakistan's judicial system, aiming to adapt to contemporary needs while preserving the foundational principles of judicial conduct.
