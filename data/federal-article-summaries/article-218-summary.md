Article 218 of the Constitution of Pakistan establishes the Election Commission, a crucial institution responsible for ensuring free and fair elections in the country. Initially, as per the 1973 Constitution, the Election Commission was constituted for each general election to the National Assembly and Provincial Assemblies. It comprised a Commissioner, who acted as the Chairman, and two members who were judges of the High Courts. The primary duty of the Election Commission was to organize and conduct elections in a manner that was honest, just, and fair, safeguarding against corrupt practices.

Over the years, this article has undergone several amendments to enhance the structure and functioning of the Election Commission. The Eighteenth Amendment in 2010 transformed the Election Commission into a permanent body, expanding its scope to include elections to both Houses of Parliament, Provincial Assemblies, and other public offices as specified by law. The composition was also altered to include four members, each from a different province, instead of two, thereby broadening the representation and oversight within the Commission. The Twentieth Amendment in 2012 made minor textual adjustments, reinforcing the Commission's duty to conduct elections fairly.

The Twenty-second Amendment in 2016 further refined the composition of the Election Commission by allowing the appointment of members who could be former judges, senior civil servants, or technocrats, provided they were not older than sixty-five years. This change aimed to bring diverse expertise into the Commission, potentially improving its effectiveness and adaptability. These amendments reflect a continuous effort to strengthen the electoral process in Pakistan, ensuring that it remains transparent and credible, which is vital for the democratic governance of the country.
