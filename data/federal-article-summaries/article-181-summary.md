Article 181 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the appointment of acting judges to the Supreme Court. This article is crucial as it ensures the continuous and efficient functioning of the Supreme Court by allowing temporary appointments when a permanent judge's position is vacant or when a judge is unable to perform their duties due to absence or other reasons. The President of Pakistan is empowered to appoint a qualified High Court judge to temporarily fill the vacancy, following the procedure outlined in Article 177. This mechanism is vital for maintaining the judiciary's ability to handle cases without interruption, thus upholding the rule of law and ensuring that justice is not delayed.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 181 has not undergone any amendments. This stability suggests that the provision has effectively served its purpose without necessitating changes. The article's unaltered state reflects a consensus on its adequacy in addressing temporary judicial vacancies, highlighting its importance in the constitutional framework. By allowing the President to make temporary appointments, the article provides a practical solution to potential disruptions in the judiciary, ensuring that the Supreme Court can continue to function smoothly and uphold its responsibilities to the citizens of Pakistan.
