Article 175-G of the Constitution of Pakistan was introduced through the Twenty-seventh Amendment on November 13, 2025, during the tenure of Asif Ali Zardari. This article establishes the Federal Constitutional Court's authority to review its own judgments or orders. The inclusion of this article signifies an important development in the judicial framework of Pakistan, as it provides a mechanism for the highest constitutional court to reassess its decisions. This power of review is subject to any legislation passed by the Parliament (Majlis-e-Shoora) and any procedural rules established by the court itself.

The introduction of Article 175-G reflects a commitment to ensuring that judicial decisions can be revisited to correct potential errors or adapt to new legal interpretations. This can enhance the flexibility and responsiveness of the judiciary, allowing it to maintain the integrity and accuracy of its rulings. The practical effect of this amendment is significant for both citizens and institutions, as it offers an additional layer of oversight and accountability within the judicial process. By allowing the Federal Constitutional Court to review its own decisions, the amendment aims to fortify the rule of law and uphold justice in the evolving legal landscape of Pakistan.
