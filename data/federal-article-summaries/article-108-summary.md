Article 108 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 establishes the roles of the Speaker and Deputy Speaker within the Provincial Assemblies. This article mandates that following a general election, the first order of business for a Provincial Assembly is to elect a Speaker and a Deputy Speaker from among its members. This process is prioritized over any other business, underscoring the importance of these roles in the legislative process. The Speaker and Deputy Speaker are crucial for maintaining order and facilitating the legislative proceedings within the Assembly. They ensure that debates are conducted fairly and that the rules of the Assembly are followed, which is vital for the democratic process and governance at the provincial level.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 108 has not been amended, indicating a consistent recognition of the importance of these positions in the legislative framework of Pakistan. The unchanging nature of this article suggests that the original provisions have been deemed effective in ensuring the smooth operation of Provincial Assemblies. By requiring the immediate election of these officials, the article helps to establish a stable and organized legislative environment, which is essential for the functioning of provincial governments and, by extension, the federal structure of Pakistan. This stability is crucial for upholding democratic principles and ensuring that provincial legislative bodies can effectively represent and address the needs of their constituents.
