Article 40 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 is focused on the country's foreign relations, particularly with Muslim countries and the broader international community. It establishes the principle that the State should work towards maintaining and enhancing fraternal ties with other Muslim nations, emphasizing the concept of Islamic unity. Additionally, the article underscores Pakistan's commitment to supporting the collective interests of the peoples of Asia, Africa, and Latin America. It also highlights the importance of promoting international peace and security, fostering goodwill and friendly relations globally, and encouraging the peaceful resolution of international disputes.

This article is significant in the constitutional framework of Pakistan as it reflects the country's foreign policy priorities and its role on the international stage. By emphasizing Islamic unity and cooperation with Muslim countries, it aligns with Pakistan's identity as an Islamic Republic. Furthermore, the article's broader focus on international peace and friendly relations underscores Pakistan's commitment to being a responsible member of the global community. This provision guides the country's diplomatic efforts and international collaborations, influencing how Pakistan interacts with other nations and participates in global forums.

Since its inclusion in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 40 has not been amended, indicating a consistent approach in Pakistan's foreign policy objectives as outlined in the Constitution. The article's enduring presence suggests that the principles it enshrines continue to be relevant and important to Pakistan's national and international identity.
