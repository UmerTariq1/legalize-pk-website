Article 141 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 establishes the legislative powers of both the federal Parliament and the Provincial Assemblies. It delineates the scope within which these legislative bodies can enact laws. Specifically, it states that the Parliament has the authority to make laws for the entire country or any part of it, including laws that have effects beyond the national borders. Similarly, each Provincial Assembly is empowered to legislate for its respective province or any part of it. This article is crucial because it sets the framework for the distribution of legislative powers between the federal and provincial levels of government, ensuring a clear demarcation of authority and preventing legislative overlap or conflict.

Since its inception, Article 141 has remained unchanged, reflecting its foundational role in the constitutional structure of Pakistan. The article's stability suggests a consensus on the importance of maintaining a clear division of legislative responsibilities between the federal and provincial governments. This division is vital for the functioning of a federal system, as it allows for regional autonomy while ensuring national coherence. By providing a mechanism for both levels of government to legislate within their respective domains, Article 141 supports the balance of power and helps accommodate the diverse needs of Pakistan's various regions.
