Article 124 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the financial management of provincial governments, specifically concerning supplementary and excess grants. This article is crucial as it provides a mechanism for provincial governments to manage unforeseen financial requirements that arise after the annual budget has been approved. It allows for additional funds to be allocated when the initially authorized budget is insufficient or when new services require funding. Furthermore, it covers situations where expenditures exceed the allocated budget for a particular service. In such cases, the provincial government is empowered to authorize the necessary expenditure from the Provincial Consolidated Fund.

The significance of Article 124 lies in its role in ensuring fiscal flexibility and accountability within provincial governance. By mandating that any supplementary or excess expenditure be presented to the Provincial Assembly, the article ensures transparency and legislative oversight. This process is essential for maintaining a balance between efficient financial management and democratic accountability, as it requires the provincial government to justify additional spending to the elected representatives of the people.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 124 has not been amended. This stability suggests that the provisions within the article have been effective in addressing the financial needs of provincial governments without necessitating changes. The article's enduring presence highlights its foundational role in the fiscal governance framework of Pakistan, ensuring that provincial governments can adapt to financial exigencies while remaining accountable to their respective assemblies.
