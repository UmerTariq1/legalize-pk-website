Article 130 of the Constitution of Pakistan, initially established in 1973, outlines the responsibilities and structure of the provincial government, particularly focusing on the relationship between the Chief Minister and the Governor. Originally, the article mandated that the Chief Minister keep the Governor informed about provincial administration and legislative proposals. This provision underscores the importance of communication and accountability within the provincial government, ensuring that the Governor, as the federal representative in the province, remains informed about provincial affairs.

The article underwent significant changes with the Eighth Amendment in 1985, which introduced provisions regarding the appointment and confidence of the Chief Minister. It allowed the Governor to appoint a Chief Minister who is likely to command the majority's confidence in the Provincial Assembly and required the Chief Minister to hold office at the Governor's pleasure, contingent upon maintaining the Assembly's confidence. These changes were reflective of the political climate under General Zia-ul-Haq, emphasizing the central government's influence over provincial matters and ensuring that provincial leadership aligned with federal expectations.

The Eighteenth Amendment in 2010 further redefined Article 130, shifting the focus to the structure and responsibilities of the provincial Cabinet. It established a Cabinet of Ministers to aid the Governor, detailed the election process for the Chief Minister, and set limits on the Cabinet's size. These changes aimed to enhance democratic processes within provincial governance, ensuring that the Chief Minister and Cabinet were more directly accountable to the Provincial Assembly. The Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025 adjusted the limits on the Cabinet's size and the number of advisers the Chief Minister could appoint, reflecting ongoing adjustments to balance governance efficiency with democratic accountability. These amendments collectively illustrate the evolving nature of provincial governance in Pakistan, adapting to changing political dynamics and governance needs.
