Article 122 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, outlines the procedure for handling the Annual Budget Statement within the Provincial Assembly. It establishes a framework for how budgetary expenditures are to be discussed and approved. Specifically, it distinguishes between expenditures charged upon the Provincial Consolidated Fund, which can be discussed but not voted on by the Assembly, and other expenditures, which must be submitted to the Assembly in the form of demands for grants. The Assembly has the authority to approve, reject, or reduce these demands. This article is crucial as it delineates the financial powers and responsibilities of the Provincial Assembly, ensuring a structured approach to provincial financial management and accountability.

The original text of Article 122, as enacted in 1973, included a provision that for a period of ten years or until the second general election, demands for grants would automatically be considered approved unless explicitly rejected or reduced by a majority vote. This provision was likely intended to provide stability and continuity in financial governance during the early years of the new constitutional framework. However, this specific provision was removed by the Eighteenth Amendment in 2010. The removal of this automatic assent clause reflects a shift towards enhancing the legislative oversight and accountability of provincial financial matters, empowering the Provincial Assembly with greater control over budgetary approvals without the automatic approval mechanism. This change underscores a broader trend in Pakistan's constitutional evolution towards strengthening democratic processes and institutional checks and balances.
