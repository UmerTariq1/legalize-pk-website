Article 145 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, establishes the power of the President to direct a provincial Governor to act as his agent in certain matters. Specifically, it allows the President to instruct a Governor to perform functions related to areas within the Federation that are not part of any province. This provision is significant because it provides a mechanism for the federal government to exercise authority in regions that fall outside the jurisdiction of provincial governments, ensuring that these areas are governed effectively and in accordance with federal policies.

The article also specifies that the usual provisions governing the Governor's functions, as outlined in Article 105, do not apply when the Governor is acting under the President's direction in this capacity. Article 105 generally pertains to the Governor's role in the provincial context, including acting on the advice of the provincial cabinet. By exempting the Governor from these provisions when acting as the President's agent, Article 145 ensures that the Governor's actions in this role are directly aligned with federal interests, rather than provincial ones.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 145 has not been amended, indicating that the original framework for the President's authority over non-provincial areas has remained intact. This stability suggests that the provision has effectively served its purpose without necessitating changes, reflecting a consistent approach to managing federal oversight in areas not governed by provincial administrations. The article plays a crucial role in maintaining the balance of power between the federal and provincial governments, ensuring that the President can directly manage certain functions in areas that require federal intervention.
