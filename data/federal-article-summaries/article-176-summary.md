Article 176 of the Constitution of Pakistan establishes the composition of the Supreme Court, the highest judicial authority in the country. Initially, as per the 1973 Constitution, the Supreme Court was to consist of a Chief Justice, referred to as the Chief Justice of Pakistan, along with other judges whose number would be determined by an Act of Parliament or, in the absence of such determination, by the President. This provision is crucial as it outlines the structure of the judiciary, ensuring that there is a clear framework for the appointment and number of judges, which is essential for maintaining the rule of law and the independence of the judiciary in Pakistan.

The article underwent a significant change with the Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025, during the tenure of Asif Ali Zardari. The amendment altered the title of the Chief Justice from "Chief Justice of Pakistan" to "Chief Justice of Supreme Court." While this change might seem minor, it reflects a shift in the formal nomenclature of the judiciary's leadership. The practical effect of this amendment on citizens or the functioning of the judiciary is largely symbolic, as it does not alter the powers or responsibilities of the Chief Justice. However, such changes can sometimes indicate broader political or institutional shifts, possibly aimed at redefining or clarifying the roles within the judiciary.
