Article 43 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 outlines the conditions related to the office of the President. This article is crucial as it establishes the rules ensuring the President's independence and impartiality, which are vital for maintaining the integrity of the highest office in the country. Specifically, it stipulates that the President cannot hold any other office of profit within the service of Pakistan, nor can they occupy any position that offers remuneration for services rendered. This provision is designed to prevent conflicts of interest and ensure that the President's duties are not influenced by other financial or professional obligations.

Additionally, Article 43 prohibits the President from being a candidate for election to either the national Parliament or any Provincial Assembly. If an individual who is already a member of Parliament or a Provincial Assembly is elected as President, their seat in the legislative body becomes vacant upon assuming the presidency. This rule underscores the separation of powers, ensuring that the President remains above partisan politics and does not hold dual roles that could compromise their ability to serve as a neutral head of state.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 43 has not been amended, indicating a consistent recognition of the importance of these conditions in preserving the non-partisan and independent nature of the presidency. By maintaining these stipulations, the article helps to uphold the constitutional framework that supports a balanced and fair governance structure in Pakistan.
