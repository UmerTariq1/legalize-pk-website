Article 79 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, is concerned with the financial management of the country's federal resources. Specifically, it addresses the custody and management of the Federal Consolidated Fund and the Public Account of the Federation. These are crucial components of the government's financial structure, as they encompass all revenues received by the federal government, loans raised, and all moneys received in repayment of any loan. The article mandates that the handling of these funds, including their deposit and withdrawal, should be governed by an Act of Parliament. Until such legislation is enacted, the President is empowered to establish rules for their management.

This article is significant because it establishes a framework for financial accountability and transparency in the federal government's operations. By requiring parliamentary oversight and regulation, it ensures that there is a democratic check on how public funds are managed and utilized. This is essential for maintaining public trust and ensuring that government resources are used effectively and for the intended purposes.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 79 has not been amended, indicating that its original provisions have been deemed sufficient to meet the needs of Pakistan's financial governance. The lack of amendments suggests a consensus on the importance of maintaining a stable and consistent framework for the management of federal funds, reflecting the foundational role this article plays in the country's constitutional and financial structure.
