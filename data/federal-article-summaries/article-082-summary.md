Article 82 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 outlines the procedure for handling the Annual Budget Statement within the National Assembly. This article is crucial as it delineates the financial powers and responsibilities of the National Assembly concerning the federal budget, which is a key aspect of parliamentary democracy and governance. The article distinguishes between two types of expenditures: those charged upon the Federal Consolidated Fund and other expenditures. The former can be discussed but not voted upon by the National Assembly, ensuring that certain essential expenditures are protected from political maneuvering. The latter type of expenditure is presented as demands for grants, which the Assembly can approve, reject, or approve with modifications, thus providing a mechanism for parliamentary oversight and control over government spending.

The article also included a transitional provision for the first ten years or until the second general election, whichever was later. During this period, demands for grants were automatically deemed approved unless actively rejected or modified by a majority vote. This provision likely aimed to ensure stability and continuity in government operations during the early years of the new constitutional framework, reflecting the political context of the time when the country was transitioning from military to civilian rule under Zulfikar Ali Bhutto's leadership.

Overall, Article 82 establishes a balance between government authority and parliamentary oversight in financial matters, which is fundamental to the functioning of a democratic state. It ensures that while the government can propose expenditures, the elected representatives have the power to scrutinize and influence financial decisions, thereby holding the government accountable to the people.
