Article 265 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 is primarily concerned with the formal title and the commencement of the Constitution itself. It establishes that the official name of the document is the "Constitution of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan." This article is crucial as it marks the legal and formal beginning of the constitutional framework that governs Pakistan. The Constitution came into force on August 14, 1973, a symbolic date that aligns with Pakistan's Independence Day, underscoring the significance of the Constitution as a foundational legal document for the nation.

The article also outlines the transitional provisions necessary for the Constitution's implementation. It specifies that certain essential functions, such as the formation of the first Senate, the initial meetings of legislative bodies, and the elections of key offices like the President and Prime Minister, could commence upon the enactment of the Constitution. However, the individuals elected to these offices would not assume their roles until the official commencement date. This ensured a smooth transition from the previous legal framework to the new constitutional order, allowing for the necessary administrative and procedural arrangements to be made in advance.

Furthermore, Article 265 provides for the exercise of powers related to the enforcement of constitutional provisions, the establishment of courts, and the appointment of judges and officers even before the Constitution officially commenced. This flexibility was vital for setting up the necessary legal and institutional structures to support the new constitutional regime. Overall, this article played a key role in facilitating the orderly transition to the new constitutional system, ensuring that the governance structures were in place and functional from the outset.
