Article 170 of the Constitution of Pakistan, as originally enacted in 1973, established the authority of the Auditor-General to prescribe the form and principles for keeping the accounts of the Federation and the Provinces, with the approval of the President. This article is significant because it ensures a standardized and regulated approach to financial record-keeping across different levels of government, which is crucial for maintaining transparency and accountability in public finances. The role of the Auditor-General is pivotal in overseeing the financial integrity of government operations, thereby contributing to good governance and public trust.

The article underwent a significant change with the Eighteenth Amendment in 2010. This amendment expanded the scope of the Auditor-General's responsibilities by adding a new provision. It mandated that the Auditor-General not only prescribes the form of accounts but also conducts audits of the accounts of both Federal and Provincial Governments, as well as any authority or body established by or under their control. The amendment empowered the Auditor-General to determine the extent and nature of these audits. This change was part of a broader effort to enhance the autonomy and effectiveness of the Auditor-General's office, ensuring that financial audits are comprehensive and independent. The practical effect of this amendment was to strengthen financial oversight and accountability mechanisms, which are essential for combating corruption and ensuring that public funds are used efficiently and effectively.
