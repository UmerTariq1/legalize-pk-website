Article 267-B of the Constitution of Pakistan, introduced through the Eighteenth Amendment in 2010, serves a clarifying function within the constitutional framework. Its primary purpose is to address and eliminate any uncertainties regarding the status of certain constitutional changes made by the Seventeenth Amendment in 2003. Specifically, it confirms that the omission of Article 152A and the substitution of Articles 179 and 195, as enacted by the Seventeenth Amendment, are to be considered as having always been in effect, even after the repeal of the Seventeenth Amendment. This provision ensures continuity and legal certainty by affirming that these changes remain valid and are not affected by the repeal of the amendment that initially introduced them.

The introduction of Article 267-B was a necessary step to prevent any legal ambiguities that might arise from the repeal of the Seventeenth Amendment. By explicitly stating that the changes made by the Seventeenth Amendment are to be regarded as permanent, the Eighteenth Amendment sought to stabilize the legal landscape and avoid potential disputes or confusion over the status of these constitutional provisions. This article underscores the importance of clarity and consistency in constitutional law, ensuring that past amendments continue to have their intended effect, thereby maintaining the integrity of the legal system in Pakistan.
