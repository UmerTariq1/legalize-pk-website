Article 159 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 addresses the distribution of responsibilities between the Federal and Provincial Governments concerning broadcasting and telecasting. Its primary purpose is to outline the conditions under which the Federal Government can delegate broadcasting functions to Provincial Governments. This article is significant because it delineates the balance of power between the central and regional authorities in the realm of media operations, which is crucial for maintaining both national cohesion and regional autonomy in media affairs.

The article stipulates that the Federal Government should not unreasonably withhold the delegation of broadcasting functions to Provincial Governments, allowing them to construct and use transmitters and regulate their use within their territories. However, it also ensures that the Federal Government retains control over transmitters and receiving apparatuses that it constructs or authorizes. This balance is essential to ensure that while provinces have the autonomy to manage local broadcasting, the Federal Government can maintain oversight to prevent any misuse that might threaten national security or unity.

Furthermore, Article 159 provides a mechanism for resolving disputes between the Federal and Provincial Governments regarding the delegation of broadcasting functions. If disagreements arise over the conditions imposed by the Federal Government or its refusal to delegate functions, an arbitrator appointed by the Chief Justice of Pakistan will resolve the issue. This provision ensures that there is a fair and impartial process for addressing conflicts, which is vital for maintaining cooperative federalism. The article also underscores that the Federal Government's powers to prevent threats to national peace and tranquility are not restricted, highlighting the importance of national security in the regulation of broadcasting and telecasting.
