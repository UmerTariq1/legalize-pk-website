Article 201 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, establishes the principle that decisions made by a High Court are binding on all subordinate courts within its jurisdiction. This means that when a High Court makes a decision on a legal question or establishes a legal principle, lower courts must follow that decision in similar cases. This article is crucial for maintaining consistency and uniformity in the application of law across the judicial system. By ensuring that subordinate courts adhere to the legal interpretations and principles set by the High Courts, the article helps create a coherent legal framework and prevents contradictory rulings within the same jurisdiction.

The article has remained unchanged since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, indicating its foundational role in the judicial hierarchy of Pakistan. The stability of this provision underscores its importance in ensuring that legal precedents are respected and followed, which is essential for the rule of law and the predictability of legal outcomes. The unamended status of Article 201 suggests that it has been effective in its purpose and has not required modification to adapt to changing legal or political contexts. This consistency supports the efficient functioning of the judiciary by providing clear guidance to subordinate courts and reinforcing the authority of High Court decisions.
