Article 162 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, establishes a crucial procedural requirement concerning fiscal legislation that impacts provincial interests. Specifically, it mandates that any bill or amendment related to taxation, which affects the financial interests of the provinces, cannot be introduced in the National Assembly without the prior approval of the President. This includes legislation that imposes or alters taxes or duties whose proceeds are allocated to provinces, changes the definition of "agricultural income" for income tax purposes, or affects the distribution of funds to provinces as outlined in the Constitution.

The purpose of this article is to ensure a balance between federal and provincial interests in fiscal matters. By requiring the President's sanction, it provides a check against unilateral federal legislative actions that could potentially undermine provincial financial autonomy or disrupt the agreed-upon distribution of resources. This safeguard is significant in maintaining the federal structure of Pakistan, where both the central government and provincial governments have defined roles and responsibilities, particularly in financial matters.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 162 has not been amended. This stability suggests a continued recognition of the importance of protecting provincial interests in fiscal legislation. The requirement for presidential sanction acts as a mechanism to ensure that any changes to taxation or financial distribution are carefully considered and balanced, reflecting the collaborative spirit intended in the federal framework of Pakistan.
