Article 252 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, establishes special provisions concerning major ports and aerodromes. This article grants the President the power to temporarily suspend or modify the application of any law—whether federal or provincial—at major ports or aerodromes for a period not exceeding three months. The President can issue such directives through a public notification, specifying the date from which the law will be affected and detailing any exceptions or modifications to its application. Importantly, this power does not retroactively affect the operation of the law before the specified date.

The purpose of this article is to provide flexibility in the governance and management of critical infrastructure such as major ports and aerodromes, which are vital for the country's trade, transportation, and security. By allowing temporary suspension or modification of laws, the government can swiftly respond to emergencies, security threats, or other significant events that may require immediate legal adjustments to ensure the smooth operation of these facilities.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 252 has not been amended. This suggests that the provision has been deemed adequate in its original form to address the needs it was designed for. The article plays a crucial role in maintaining the balance between legal rigidity and the need for adaptive governance in areas of strategic importance, ensuring that the state can act decisively when necessary without permanently altering the legal landscape.
