Article 78 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, establishes the framework for managing the financial resources of the federal government. It defines two key financial entities: the Federal Consolidated Fund and the Public Account of the Federation. The Federal Consolidated Fund comprises all revenues, loans, and repayments received by the federal government, serving as the primary pool of funds for governmental expenditures. The Public Account, on the other hand, includes other moneys received by the federal government or deposited with the courts, ensuring that these funds are separately accounted for and managed. This article is crucial as it ensures transparency and accountability in the handling of public finances, which is fundamental for maintaining public trust and effective governance.

The article underwent a significant amendment with the Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025. This amendment expanded the scope of the Public Account to include funds received by or deposited with the newly established Federal Constitutional Court, in addition to the Supreme Court and other courts under the federation's authority. This change reflects an institutional evolution within Pakistan's judicial system, acknowledging the creation of a new court with constitutional significance. The practical effect of this amendment is to ensure that the financial dealings of the Federal Constitutional Court are transparently managed and accounted for, aligning with the broader principles of financial accountability and judicial independence. This amendment may have been driven by political or judicial reforms aimed at strengthening the constitutional framework and enhancing the separation of powers within the state.
