Article 175-C of the Constitution of Pakistan, introduced through the Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025, establishes the framework for the appointment of judges to the Federal Constitutional Court. This article is significant as it outlines the qualifications and process for appointing these judges, thereby ensuring that the judiciary is composed of individuals with substantial legal experience and expertise. The Federal Constitutional Court plays a crucial role in interpreting the Constitution and adjudicating constitutional disputes, making the appointment of its judges a matter of national importance.

The article specifies that the Chief Justice and other judges of the Federal Constitutional Court are to be appointed by the President of Pakistan, following the procedures outlined in Article 175A. To qualify for appointment, a person must be a citizen of Pakistan and meet one of the following criteria: they must either be a current or former judge of the Supreme Court, have served as a judge of a High Court for at least seven years, or have been an advocate of a High Court for at least twenty years and currently be an advocate of the Supreme Court. This ensures that only individuals with significant judicial or legal experience are eligible for these prestigious positions.

Additionally, the article addresses the issue of seniority among judges, stating that a judge of the Supreme Court will maintain their seniority relative to other judges, except for the Chief Justice of the Federal Constitutional Court, regardless of when they are appointed. This provision helps maintain the hierarchy and order within the judiciary, which is essential for its smooth functioning. The introduction of this article reflects a structured approach to judicial appointments, aiming to enhance the integrity and effectiveness of the constitutional judiciary in Pakistan.
