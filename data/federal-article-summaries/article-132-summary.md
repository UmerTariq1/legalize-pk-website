Article 132 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 establishes the framework for the appointment and functioning of Provincial Ministers within the provincial government. Initially, it laid out the process by which the Chief Minister appoints Provincial Ministers from the members of the Provincial Assembly. It also specified that these ministers must take an oath before the Governor before assuming office. Furthermore, it provided the mechanism for a minister to resign or be removed from office, with the resignation being addressed to the Chief Minister.

The article underwent a significant change with the Eighteenth Amendment in 2010. This amendment introduced a reference to additional clauses (9) and (10), which were not part of the original text, indicating that the appointment of Provincial Ministers must now comply with these new provisions. Although the specific content of clauses (9) and (10) is not detailed in the diff, their inclusion suggests a move towards more structured or possibly restrictive criteria for the appointment of ministers, reflecting a shift towards greater regulation and oversight in provincial governance. This change likely aimed to enhance accountability and ensure that appointments are made following a more defined legal framework, thereby impacting how provincial governments operate and potentially influencing the political dynamics within provincial assemblies.
