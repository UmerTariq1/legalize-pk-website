Article 93 of the Constitution of Pakistan, as originally enacted in 1973, addresses the continuity of the Prime Minister's tenure. It ensures that the Prime Minister remains in office until a successor is duly appointed and assumes the role. This provision is crucial for maintaining governmental stability and continuity, especially during transitions between administrations. Additionally, the article clarifies that the dissolution of the National Assembly does not disqualify the Prime Minister, Federal Ministers, or Ministers of State from continuing in their roles. This aspect is significant as it prevents a power vacuum and ensures that the executive branch can continue to function effectively even when the legislative body is not in session.

The article underwent a change with the Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025, although the diff does not indicate any substantive alteration to the text itself. The amendment's documentation suggests a reaffirmation or administrative update rather than a change in the article's provisions. The lack of textual change implies that the principles of continuity and stability in governance, as originally intended, remain intact. This continuity is essential for the political stability of Pakistan, ensuring that executive functions are not disrupted during periods of legislative transition or dissolution.
