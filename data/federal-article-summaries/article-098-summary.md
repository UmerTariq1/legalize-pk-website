Article 98 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 is concerned with the delegation of functions to subordinate authorities. Specifically, it allows Parliament, upon the recommendation of the Federal Government, to pass laws that assign certain duties or responsibilities to officers or authorities that are subordinate to the Federal Government. This provision is significant because it provides a mechanism for the Federal Government to efficiently manage and execute its functions by empowering lower-level authorities to carry out specific tasks. This can help in decentralizing administrative responsibilities and ensuring that government functions are carried out more effectively and responsively.

The article has remained unchanged since its inclusion in the original 1973 Constitution, indicating that its purpose and function have been consistently deemed appropriate and necessary for the governance structure of Pakistan. By allowing the Federal Government to delegate tasks, this article supports a flexible and adaptive administrative framework, which can be crucial for addressing the diverse and evolving needs of the country. The ability to confer functions on subordinate authorities ensures that the Federal Government can maintain oversight while also empowering local authorities to act within their jurisdictions, thereby promoting efficiency and responsiveness in governance.
