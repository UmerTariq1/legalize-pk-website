Article 205 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, concerns the remuneration and other terms and conditions of service for judges of the Supreme Court and High Courts. This article is crucial as it establishes the financial and service-related framework for the judiciary, ensuring that judges are compensated and treated in a manner that upholds the independence and integrity of the judicial system. By linking these terms to the Fifth Schedule, the Constitution provides a structured and transparent mechanism for determining judicial remuneration, which is essential for maintaining the separation of powers and preventing undue influence on the judiciary.

Originally, the article specified the remuneration and service conditions for judges of the Supreme Court and High Courts. However, it was amended by the Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025, during the tenure of Asif Ali Zardari. This amendment expanded the scope of the article to include judges of the newly established Federal Constitutional Court, in addition to the Supreme Court and High Courts. The practical effect of this change was to ensure that judges of the Federal Constitutional Court, a court likely created to address constitutional issues more directly, are also covered under the same financial and service terms as other high-ranking judges. This amendment reflects an evolution in Pakistan's judicial structure, possibly in response to a need for more specialized constitutional adjudication, and underscores the importance of maintaining consistent standards across all high-level judicial appointments.
