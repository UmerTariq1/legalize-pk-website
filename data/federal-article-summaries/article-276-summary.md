Article 276 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 deals with a specific procedural matter concerning the oath-taking of the first President of Pakistan under the new constitutional framework established in 1973. This article provides a contingency plan for the administration of the presidential oath, stating that if the Chief Justice of Pakistan is unavailable, the first President can take the oath before the Speaker of the National Assembly. This provision ensures that the inauguration of the first President under the new constitution could proceed smoothly without being delayed due to the absence of the Chief Justice.

The article was part of the original 1973 Constitution and has not been amended since its inception. Its inclusion reflects the practical considerations of establishing a new constitutional order and ensuring that the transition of power could occur without procedural hindrances. By allowing the Speaker of the National Assembly to administer the oath, the framers of the Constitution provided a flexible solution to potential logistical issues, underscoring the importance of continuity and stability in the nascent stages of the constitutional regime. This article, while specific in its application, highlights the foresight of the constitutional drafters in addressing potential challenges in the implementation of the new system of governance.
