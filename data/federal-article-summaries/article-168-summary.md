Article 168 of the Constitution of Pakistan establishes the office of the Auditor-General of Pakistan, a crucial role responsible for auditing the accounts of the federal and provincial governments to ensure transparency and accountability in public financial management. The Auditor-General is appointed by the President and is required to take an oath before the Chief Justice of Pakistan, underscoring the importance and independence of the position. This role is vital in the constitutional structure as it provides an independent check on the financial operations of the government, helping to prevent misuse of public funds and promoting good governance.

Originally, the terms and conditions of the Auditor-General's service, including the term of office, were to be determined by an Act of Parliament or, until such legislation was enacted, by an order of the President. The Eighteenth Amendment in 2010 brought significant changes to this article. It specified that the Auditor-General would hold office for a term of four years or until reaching the age of sixty-five, whichever came first, unless they resigned or were removed under specific conditions. This amendment aimed to provide clarity and stability to the tenure of the Auditor-General, ensuring that the officeholder could perform their duties without undue influence or pressure.

Additionally, the amendment refined the process for appointing an acting Auditor-General in case of a vacancy or the incumbent's inability to perform their duties. Instead of the President appointing any person, the amendment stipulated that the most senior officer in the Office of the Auditor-General would assume the role. This change was likely intended to maintain continuity and uphold the integrity of the office by ensuring that someone with relevant experience and understanding of the office's operations would temporarily fill the position. These amendments reflect a broader effort to strengthen institutional frameworks and enhance the independence and effectiveness of the Auditor-General's office in Pakistan.
