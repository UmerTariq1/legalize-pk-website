Article 230 of the Constitution of Pakistan outlines the functions of the Islamic Council, a body tasked with ensuring that the laws and practices in Pakistan align with Islamic principles as outlined in the Quran and Sunnah. The Council's primary responsibilities include advising the Parliament and Provincial Assemblies on how to encourage Muslims to live according to Islamic principles, assessing whether proposed laws are in conflict with Islamic injunctions, and recommending changes to existing laws to bring them into conformity with Islamic teachings. This article is significant as it institutionalizes the role of Islam in the legislative process, reflecting the importance of religion in Pakistan's constitutional framework.

Originally established in the 1973 Constitution, the article has undergone one amendment, the Twenty-sixth Amendment in 2024. This amendment introduced a provision that mandates the final report of the Islamic Council to be considered within twelve months after it is laid before the legislative bodies. This change aims to ensure timely consideration and potential implementation of the Council's recommendations, thereby enhancing the efficiency and responsiveness of the legislative process in aligning with Islamic principles. The amendment reflects a political commitment to uphold the influence of Islamic guidance in governance, potentially driven by the need to address delays in legislative processes and ensure that the Council's work has a tangible impact on lawmaking.
