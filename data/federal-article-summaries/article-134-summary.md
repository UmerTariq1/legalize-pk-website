Article 134 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, outlines the procedure for the resignation of a Chief Minister. It establishes the protocol that the Chief Minister of a province may resign by submitting a written notice to the Governor. Upon the resignation of the Chief Minister, all Provincial Ministers automatically cease to hold office. This article is significant as it ensures a clear and orderly transition of power within the provincial government, maintaining stability and continuity in governance.

The article also provides for the continuity of governance by allowing the resigning Chief Minister and any Provincial Ministers, upon the Chief Minister's request, to continue performing their duties until a new Chief Minister is elected and assumes office. This provision is crucial as it prevents any administrative vacuum that could disrupt the functioning of the provincial government. Furthermore, if the Provincial Assembly is in session at the time of the resignation, it is required to immediately elect a new Chief Minister. If the Assembly is not in session, the Governor must summon it to meet within fourteen days to elect a new Chief Minister. This ensures that the process of electing a new leader is prompt and efficient, minimizing any potential political instability.

Since its inclusion in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 134 has not been amended. This suggests that the procedures it establishes have been effective in managing the resignation and succession of Chief Ministers in Pakistan's provinces. The article plays a vital role in maintaining the democratic process at the provincial level, ensuring that leadership transitions are conducted smoothly and in accordance with constitutional guidelines.
