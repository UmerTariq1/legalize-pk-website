Article 280 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 addresses the continuity and legal validation of the Proclamation of Emergency that was issued on November 23, 1971. This article is significant as it retroactively legitimizes the emergency measures taken during that period by deeming them to have been issued under Article 232 of the Constitution. The importance of this article lies in its role in ensuring that any laws, rules, or orders made under the emergency proclamation are considered valid, thereby preventing any legal challenges to their legitimacy. This provision is crucial in maintaining the continuity of governance and legal order during times of national crisis.

The article underwent a significant amendment with the Fifth Amendment on September 16, 1976. This amendment added a crucial clause stating that the laws, rules, or orders made under the Proclamation of Emergency cannot be questioned in any court on the grounds of inconsistency with the rights conferred by Chapter I of Part II of the Constitution. This change effectively shielded emergency measures from judicial review concerning fundamental rights, thereby strengthening the government's ability to implement and maintain emergency provisions without legal hindrance. The political context of this amendment likely reflects the government's desire to consolidate power and ensure stability during a period marked by political and social challenges.
