Article 150 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, establishes the principle of "full faith and credit" across the country. This means that public acts, records, and judicial proceedings from one province must be recognized and respected in all other provinces. The purpose of this article is to ensure legal uniformity and cohesion across the diverse regions of Pakistan, facilitating smoother inter-provincial interactions and legal processes. By mandating that each province honors the legal documents and judicial decisions of others, the article helps maintain national unity and prevents legal conflicts that could arise from provincial discrepancies.

Since its inception, Article 150 has not been amended, indicating its foundational importance and the enduring relevance of its principles. The unaltered nature of this article suggests a consensus on the necessity of maintaining a consistent legal framework across the provinces. This consistency is crucial for citizens who move between provinces, as it assures them that their legal rights and obligations will be recognized uniformly, regardless of provincial boundaries. By fostering legal predictability and stability, Article 150 plays a vital role in supporting the rule of law and the seamless operation of Pakistan's federal system.
