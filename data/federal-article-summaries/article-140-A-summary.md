Article 140-A of the Constitution of Pakistan was introduced through the Eighteenth Amendment in 2010. This article is significant as it mandates the establishment of a local government system in each province. The purpose of this article is to ensure that political, administrative, and financial responsibilities and authorities are devolved to elected representatives at the local level. This devolution is crucial for enhancing democratic governance and ensuring that decision-making is more responsive to the needs of local communities. By empowering local governments, the article aims to bring government closer to the people, thereby improving service delivery and accountability.

The introduction of Article 140-A was a pivotal step in strengthening the democratic framework of Pakistan. Prior to this amendment, local governance was often subject to the discretion of provincial governments, which could lead to inconsistencies and a lack of continuity in local administration. By constitutionally mandating the establishment of local governments, the Eighteenth Amendment sought to create a more stable and consistent framework for local governance across the country. Furthermore, the article assigns the responsibility of conducting local government elections to the Election Commission of Pakistan, ensuring that these elections are held in a fair and transparent manner.

The practical effect of Article 140-A has been to provide a constitutional guarantee for local governance, which is essential for fostering grassroots democracy. It empowers citizens by allowing them to elect representatives who are directly accountable to them, thereby enhancing civic participation and local accountability. This change reflects a broader political commitment to decentralization and democratization, aiming to address the diverse needs of Pakistan's population more effectively by involving them directly in governance processes.
