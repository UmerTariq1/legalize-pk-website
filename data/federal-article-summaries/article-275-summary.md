Article 275 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the continuity of service for individuals who were already serving in various capacities within the government at the time the Constitution came into effect. This provision was crucial for ensuring a smooth transition from the Interim Constitution to the new constitutional framework established in 1973. It guaranteed that those in service, including high-ranking officials such as judges, governors, chief ministers, and other key officeholders, would continue in their roles under the same terms and conditions as before. This continuity was essential to maintain stability and prevent any disruption in the functioning of the government and judiciary during the transition.

The article also made specific provisions for certain officeholders, such as Federal Ministers and Provincial Ministers, allowing them to serve even if they were not members of the Parliament or Provincial Assembly for a limited period of six months. This was a pragmatic measure to ensure that experienced individuals could continue to contribute to governance without immediate electoral constraints. Additionally, it required individuals holding certain offices to take an oath as prescribed in the Third Schedule of the Constitution, reinforcing the commitment to the new constitutional order.

Furthermore, Article 275 ensured that all courts and authorities, whether judicial, executive, or ministerial, would continue to function as they did before the Constitution's commencement. This clause was vital for maintaining the rule of law and the uninterrupted administration of justice and governance across Pakistan. Overall, Article 275 played a significant role in facilitating the transition to the new constitutional regime by preserving institutional continuity and stability.
