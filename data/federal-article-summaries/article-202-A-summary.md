Article 202-A of the Constitution of Pakistan establishes the creation of Constitutional Benches within the High Courts. These benches are composed of judges nominated by the Judicial Commission of Pakistan, which is a body responsible for the appointment and oversight of judges. The purpose of these benches is to handle specific constitutional matters, ensuring that cases of significant constitutional importance are addressed by a specialized group of judges. This article is crucial as it aims to streamline the judicial process for constitutional cases, potentially leading to more consistent and expert adjudication of such matters.

The article was first introduced through the Twenty-sixth Amendment in October 2024. It specified that only the Constitutional Benches could exercise jurisdiction over certain constitutional matters as outlined in Article 199 of the Constitution. This amendment aimed to centralize and specialize the handling of constitutional cases within the High Courts, potentially improving the efficiency and quality of judicial decisions in these complex areas. The Twenty-seventh Amendment, enacted in November 2025, further refined this by simplifying the jurisdictional language, stating that no other bench could exercise jurisdiction under Article 199, thus broadening the scope of cases that the Constitutional Benches could handle. This change likely aimed to reduce ambiguity and ensure that all relevant constitutional cases were directed to these specialized benches, enhancing the consistency and expertise applied to constitutional adjudication.
