Article 175-H, introduced through the Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025, establishes the advisory jurisdiction of the Federal Constitutional Court in Pakistan. This article allows the President of Pakistan to seek the opinion of the Federal Constitutional Court on any legal question deemed to be of public importance. The purpose of this provision is to enable the President to obtain a judicial perspective on complex legal issues that may impact the nation, thereby ensuring that decisions are informed by legal expertise and are in alignment with constitutional principles.

The introduction of Article 175-H reflects an effort to formalize a mechanism for resolving significant legal questions that could affect public policy or governance. By allowing the President to refer such questions to the Federal Constitutional Court, the amendment aims to enhance the role of the judiciary in providing guidance on constitutional matters. This could potentially lead to more consistent and legally sound decision-making at the highest levels of government, fostering stability and public confidence in the legal system.

The practical effect of this amendment is significant, as it empowers the judiciary to play a proactive role in shaping the interpretation of laws and constitutional provisions. It also underscores the importance of the separation of powers, as it provides a structured way for the executive branch to seek judicial input without overstepping its bounds. This development could be seen as a move towards strengthening the rule of law in Pakistan, ensuring that legal interpretations are not solely the domain of the executive but are informed by the judiciary's expertise.
