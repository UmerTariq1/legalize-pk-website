Article 187 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 deals with the powers of the Supreme Court to issue directions, orders, or decrees necessary for ensuring complete justice in cases before it. This article is significant as it empowers the Supreme Court to take necessary actions to ensure justice is served, including compelling attendance or the production of documents. The enforceability of these orders throughout Pakistan underscores the central role of the Supreme Court in maintaining the rule of law and ensuring uniformity in judicial processes across the country.

The article has undergone several amendments that have refined its scope and application. The Fifth Amendment in 1976 introduced a reference to Article 175, which pertains to the jurisdiction of the courts, thereby linking the Supreme Court's powers to the broader judicial framework. This change was likely intended to ensure that the Supreme Court's powers were exercised within the established jurisdictional boundaries, reflecting a balance between judicial authority and constitutional limits.

In 2024, the Twenty-sixth Amendment added a proviso that orders must be made in pursuance of jurisdiction vested in the Supreme Court, further emphasizing the need for judicial actions to be grounded in legitimate authority. The most recent change, the Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025, expanded the article to include the Federal Constitutional Court alongside the Supreme Court, indicating a structural change in the judicial system. This amendment suggests a political or institutional shift towards a more complex judicial framework, possibly to address evolving legal and constitutional challenges in Pakistan. These amendments collectively illustrate an ongoing effort to refine the judicial process and ensure that the powers of the courts are exercised responsibly and effectively.
