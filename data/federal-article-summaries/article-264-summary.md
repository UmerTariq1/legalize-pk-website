Article 264 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 addresses the consequences of repealing laws. Its primary purpose is to ensure legal continuity and stability when a law is repealed. This article stipulates that the repeal of a law does not automatically revive any previous law that was not in force at the time of repeal. It also ensures that the repeal does not affect the previous operation of the law, nor does it impact any rights, obligations, or liabilities that were acquired or incurred under the repealed law. Additionally, it safeguards any penalties or punishments that were imposed under the repealed law, and it allows for ongoing investigations or legal proceedings to continue as if the law had not been repealed.

This provision is crucial in maintaining legal certainty and protecting the rights and obligations of individuals and institutions that were established under the repealed law. By ensuring that actions taken under a repealed law remain valid, Article 264 prevents legal vacuums and ensures that justice and administrative processes are not disrupted. This continuity is vital for the rule of law, as it reassures citizens and institutions that their legal rights and responsibilities are not arbitrarily altered by the repeal of legislation.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 264 has not been amended, indicating its foundational role in the constitutional framework of Pakistan. Its unaltered state suggests a consensus on the importance of legal continuity and the protection of vested rights, which are essential for the stability and predictability of the legal system. This article underscores the commitment to uphold the rule of law and ensure that legislative changes do not retroactively disrupt established legal relationships or processes.
