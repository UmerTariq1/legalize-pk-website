Article 2 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, establishes Islam as the state religion of Pakistan. This article is foundational in defining the religious identity of the nation, reflecting the majority Muslim population's beliefs and the historical context in which the country was created. The inclusion of this article underscores the importance of Islam in the cultural, legal, and political fabric of Pakistan, influencing various aspects of governance and public life. It serves as a guiding principle for the state's laws and policies, ensuring that they align with Islamic values and principles.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 2 has not been amended, indicating a consistent commitment to maintaining Islam as the state religion. This continuity suggests a broad consensus across different political regimes and societal segments regarding the role of Islam in the state's identity. The article's unaltered status reflects its significance and the sensitivity surrounding religious matters in Pakistan. It plays a crucial role in shaping the legal framework and societal norms, impacting everything from education and family law to public holidays and the observance of religious practices.
