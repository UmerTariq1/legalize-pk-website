Article 109 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, deals with the powers of the Governor regarding the summoning and prorogation of the Provincial Assembly. This article establishes the Governor's authority to call the Provincial Assembly into session and to discontinue its meetings without dissolving it. The ability to summon and prorogue the assembly is crucial for the functioning of provincial governance, as it allows the Governor to manage the legislative calendar and ensure that the assembly meets to discuss and pass legislation as needed. This power is significant in maintaining the balance of power between the executive and legislative branches at the provincial level, ensuring that the assembly can be convened to address urgent matters or prorogued to manage legislative workload and political strategy.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 109 has not been amended. This suggests that the provisions within the article have been deemed adequate and effective in fulfilling their intended purpose. The unchanged nature of this article indicates a stable aspect of the constitutional framework concerning provincial legislative procedures. The Governor's role in summoning and proroguing the assembly remains a key function in the provincial political structure, enabling the executive to coordinate with the legislative body to address the needs and priorities of the province.
