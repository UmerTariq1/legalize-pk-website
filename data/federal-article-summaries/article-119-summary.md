Article 119 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 is concerned with the financial management of provincial funds. It outlines the rules regarding the custody and management of the Provincial Consolidated Fund and the Public Account of the Province. Essentially, this article establishes the framework for how money is to be handled by provincial governments, including how funds are deposited into and withdrawn from these accounts. The Provincial Consolidated Fund is a key financial resource for provincial governments, consisting of all revenues received by the provincial government, loans raised, and money received in repayment of loans. The Public Account, on the other hand, includes money received by the provincial government in trust or for specific purposes.

The article mandates that the regulation of these financial matters should be done through legislation passed by the Provincial Assembly. However, until such legislation is enacted, the Governor of the province is empowered to make rules to manage these funds. This provision ensures that there is always a mechanism in place for the proper management of provincial finances, which is crucial for maintaining fiscal discipline and accountability within the provincial governments. The article has not been amended since its inclusion in the original 1973 Constitution, indicating that its provisions have remained relevant and effective in guiding the financial operations of provincial governments in Pakistan. This stability reflects the foundational role that Article 119 plays in the financial governance structure of the provinces, ensuring that there is a clear and consistent process for managing public funds at the provincial level.
