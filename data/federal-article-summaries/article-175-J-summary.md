Article 175-J was introduced into the Constitution of Pakistan through the Twenty-seventh Amendment in November 2025. This article establishes the procedure for appointing an Acting Chief Justice of the Federal Constitutional Court. It specifies that if the position of Chief Justice is vacant, or if the Chief Justice is unable to perform their duties for any reason, the President of Pakistan is empowered to appoint another judge from the Federal Constitutional Court to act as the Chief Justice temporarily.

The creation of this article is significant as it ensures the continuity and stability of the Federal Constitutional Court's leadership. By providing a clear mechanism for appointing an Acting Chief Justice, the amendment addresses potential disruptions in the court's functioning that could arise from the absence or incapacity of the Chief Justice. This change reflects an effort to maintain the effective administration of justice and uphold the rule of law, which are foundational principles of Pakistan's constitutional framework. The introduction of this article may have been influenced by political considerations to ensure that the judiciary remains operational and independent, even in times of transition or unforeseen circumstances affecting the Chief Justice.
