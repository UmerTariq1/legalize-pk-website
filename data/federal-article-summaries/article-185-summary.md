Article 185 of the Constitution of Pakistan pertains to the appellate jurisdiction of the Supreme Court, which is the highest court in the country. This article outlines the circumstances under which appeals can be made to the Supreme Court from the judgments, decrees, orders, or sentences of High Courts. It is a crucial component of the judicial system as it provides a mechanism for reviewing decisions made by lower courts, ensuring that justice is served and legal errors can be corrected at the highest judicial level. This appellate jurisdiction is vital for maintaining the rule of law and protecting the rights of citizens by allowing for a higher level of scrutiny in legal proceedings.

Initially, the article, as established in the original 1973 Constitution, allowed appeals to the Supreme Court from High Court decisions only if the Supreme Court granted leave to appeal. This meant that not all cases could automatically be appealed to the Supreme Court, thereby limiting the court's caseload and ensuring that only significant or contentious cases were reviewed at the highest level.

The article underwent significant changes with the Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025. This amendment expanded the scope of cases that could be appealed to the Supreme Court without requiring leave. It specified several scenarios where an appeal could be made directly, such as cases involving the reversal of an acquittal leading to a death sentence, life imprisonment, or cases involving substantial financial disputes. These changes were likely motivated by a need to streamline the appellate process and ensure that critical cases, especially those involving severe penalties or significant financial implications, receive due consideration by the Supreme Court. This amendment reflects an effort to enhance judicial efficiency and accessibility, ensuring that the Supreme Court can address matters of substantial legal and public interest more effectively.
