Article 164 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the ability of the Federation or a Province to make financial grants from the Consolidated Fund for any purpose, even if that purpose is not within the legislative competence of the Parliament or Provincial Assembly. This provision is significant as it provides flexibility in financial governance, allowing the federal or provincial governments to allocate funds for purposes that may not be explicitly covered by existing legislative powers. This can be particularly important in addressing unforeseen circumstances or needs that arise outside the scope of current laws, thereby enabling a more responsive and adaptable governance structure.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 164 has remained unchanged, indicating a consistent recognition of the need for such financial discretion within the constitutional framework. The absence of amendments suggests that this provision has been effective in its purpose and has not required modification to address changing political or economic conditions. By allowing for such financial grants, the article ensures that both federal and provincial governments have the necessary tools to manage resources effectively and respond to the needs of their constituents, thereby playing a crucial role in the financial administration of Pakistan.
