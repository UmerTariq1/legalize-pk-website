Article 189 of the Constitution of Pakistan originally established the principle that decisions made by the Supreme Court are binding on all other courts in the country. This means that when the Supreme Court makes a decision on a legal question or establishes a legal principle, lower courts must follow that decision in similar cases. This provision is crucial as it ensures uniformity and consistency in the application of law across Pakistan, reinforcing the Supreme Court's role as the highest judicial authority in the country.

The article underwent a significant change with the Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025. This amendment introduced the Federal Constitutional Court, placing it at the apex of the judicial hierarchy concerning constitutional matters. The amendment specified that decisions of the Federal Constitutional Court are binding on all other courts, including the Supreme Court, when it comes to questions of law or legal principles. Conversely, the Supreme Court's decisions remain binding on all other courts except the Federal Constitutional Court. This change reflects a shift in the judicial structure, potentially aimed at creating a specialized body to handle constitutional issues, thereby relieving the Supreme Court of some of its burdens and possibly addressing political or legal complexities that necessitated a distinct constitutional adjudicator.
