Article 84 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the financial management of the state by providing a mechanism for supplementary and excess grants. This article is crucial as it ensures that the government can respond to unforeseen financial needs or emergencies that arise during a financial year. Specifically, it allows the Federal Government to authorize additional expenditure if the initially approved budget is insufficient or if new services require funding that was not anticipated in the Annual Budget Statement. Additionally, if any service incurs expenses beyond the allocated budget, this article permits the government to cover those excesses.

The process outlined in Article 84 requires the Federal Government to present a Supplementary Budget Statement or an Excess Budget Statement to the National Assembly. This ensures transparency and accountability, as these statements are subject to the same scrutiny and procedures as the original Annual Budget Statement, as per Articles 80 to 83 of the Constitution. By mandating this process, the article reinforces the checks and balances necessary for responsible fiscal governance, allowing the government to maintain financial stability while being adaptable to changing circumstances.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 84 has not been amended, indicating its foundational role in the financial governance framework of Pakistan. Its unaltered status suggests that the procedures it establishes have been effective in managing the country's financial exigencies without necessitating changes. This stability is significant for maintaining consistent fiscal policies and ensuring that the government can meet its financial obligations and respond to new challenges efficiently.
