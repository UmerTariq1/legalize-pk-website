Article 118 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, establishes the framework for managing the financial resources of a provincial government. It outlines the creation of two key financial accounts: the Provincial Consolidated Fund and the Public Account of the Province. The Provincial Consolidated Fund is comprised of all revenues received by the provincial government, loans raised by it, and repayments of loans. This fund is crucial as it represents the main financial reservoir from which the provincial government can draw to finance its operations and development projects. The Public Account, on the other hand, includes other moneys received by the provincial government or deposited with courts within the province. This distinction ensures a clear separation between general revenues and other types of financial transactions, promoting transparency and accountability in provincial financial management.

The article has remained unchanged since its inception in 1973, indicating its foundational role in the financial governance of provinces in Pakistan. Its unaltered state suggests that the original provisions have been effective in meeting the needs of provincial financial administration without requiring modification. By clearly delineating the sources and management of provincial funds, Article 118 plays a vital role in ensuring that provincial governments have the necessary financial autonomy and structure to manage their resources effectively. This autonomy is essential for the provinces to address local needs and priorities, thereby supporting the broader federal structure of governance in Pakistan.
