Article 260 of the Constitution of Pakistan is a crucial component that provides definitions for various terms used throughout the Constitution. This article serves as a foundational reference point, ensuring clarity and consistency in the interpretation of constitutional provisions. By defining terms such as "Act of Parliament," "citizen," "Governor," "Judge," and many others, Article 260 helps to avoid ambiguity and potential legal disputes over the meanings of these terms. This is particularly important in a legal document as significant as the Constitution, where precise language is essential for the effective functioning of government and the protection of citizens' rights.

Over the years, Article 260 has undergone several amendments, reflecting the evolving legal and political landscape of Pakistan. The First Amendment in 1974 introduced the definition of "Chief Justice" and expanded the definition of "service of Pakistan" to exclude certain high-ranking positions. The Second Amendment, also in 1974, added a clause that defined who is considered a Muslim for constitutional purposes, a change that had significant social and political implications, particularly for religious minorities. Subsequent amendments, such as the Fifth and Sixth, further refined the definition of "service of Pakistan" by excluding additional roles like the Advocate-General and various advisory positions.

The most recent amendments, including the Eighteenth and Twenty-seventh, have continued to update and expand the definitions within Article 260. Notably, the Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025 introduced the term "technocrat," reflecting the increasing importance of expertise and experience in governance. These changes illustrate the dynamic nature of constitutional law in Pakistan, as the government seeks to address contemporary challenges and ensure that the Constitution remains relevant and effective in guiding the nation's legal framework.
