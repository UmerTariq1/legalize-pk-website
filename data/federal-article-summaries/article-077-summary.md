Article 77 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 establishes a fundamental principle regarding taxation within the country. It mandates that no tax can be imposed for the purposes of the Federation unless it is done so through an Act of Parliament. This article is crucial as it ensures that the power to levy taxes is firmly placed within the legislative branch of the government, thereby upholding the democratic process and ensuring that taxation is subject to parliamentary scrutiny and approval. This provision is designed to prevent arbitrary taxation by the executive branch and to ensure that any tax imposed is debated and agreed upon by the representatives of the people.

The article has not been amended since its inception, indicating a consistent adherence to the principle that taxation should be a matter of legislative authority. This stability reflects the importance of maintaining a clear and democratic process for tax legislation, which is a cornerstone of fiscal policy and governance. By requiring parliamentary approval for taxation, the article protects citizens from potential overreach by the government and ensures transparency and accountability in how public funds are raised and utilized. This provision is a key element in the constitutional framework, reinforcing the role of Parliament in financial matters and safeguarding the economic rights of the citizens.
