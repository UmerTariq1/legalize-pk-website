Article 244 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 is concerned with the oath-taking process for members of the Armed Forces. This article establishes the requirement that every member of the military must take an oath as prescribed in the Third Schedule of the Constitution. The purpose of this oath is to ensure that all military personnel pledge their allegiance to the state and commit to upholding the Constitution. This is a crucial element in maintaining the integrity and loyalty of the Armed Forces, ensuring that they operate under the constitutional framework and remain apolitical and subordinate to civilian authority.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 244 has not been amended. This indicates a consistent recognition of the importance of a formal oath in maintaining the discipline and constitutional alignment of the Armed Forces. The unaltered nature of this article suggests that the foundational principles it embodies have remained relevant and uncontested over the years. By requiring an oath, the article reinforces the Armed Forces' role in safeguarding the nation's sovereignty while adhering to the rule of law and democratic principles enshrined in the Constitution.
