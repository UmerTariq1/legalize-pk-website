Article 116 of the Constitution of Pakistan outlines the process by which a bill passed by a Provincial Assembly becomes law, specifically focusing on the role of the Governor in granting assent. Initially, the article mandated that once a bill was passed by a Provincial Assembly, it had to be presented to the Governor, who was required to assent to it within seven days. If the Governor did not act within this period, the bill was automatically deemed to have been assented to, thus becoming law. This provision ensured a streamlined legislative process at the provincial level, preventing undue delays in the enactment of laws.

The article has undergone significant changes through constitutional amendments. The Eighth Amendment in 1985 extended the period for the Governor's assent from seven to thirty days, allowing more time for consideration. Additionally, it introduced a mechanism for the Governor to return a bill to the Provincial Assembly for reconsideration. If the Assembly passed the bill again, the Governor was then required to assent to it. This change provided a check on the Provincial Assembly's power, allowing the Governor to request a review of legislation, which could be seen as a move to strengthen the executive's influence over provincial legislation during a period of military rule under General Zia-ul-Haq.

The Eighteenth Amendment in 2010 further refined this process by reducing the time for the Governor's assent from thirty days to ten days, thereby expediting the legislative process. It also stipulated that if a bill was returned and then passed again by the Assembly, the Governor had to give assent within ten days, or it would be deemed to have been given. This amendment was part of a broader effort to enhance provincial autonomy and streamline governance, reflecting a shift towards strengthening democratic institutions and reducing potential executive overreach. These changes have had a practical impact by ensuring that provincial legislation is enacted more efficiently, thereby enhancing the responsiveness of provincial governments to the needs of their constituents.
