Article 38 of the Constitution of Pakistan is focused on the promotion of social justice and the eradication of social evils. It outlines the responsibilities of the State to ensure the well-being of its citizens by improving their standard of living and preventing the concentration of wealth in the hands of a few. The article mandates the State to provide facilities for work, adequate livelihood, social security, and basic necessities such as food, clothing, housing, education, and medical relief. It also emphasizes reducing income disparities and eliminating riba (interest) to promote economic equity. This article is significant as it sets the foundation for social welfare policies and economic justice, aiming to create a more equitable society.

The article was first introduced in the original 1973 Constitution and underwent its first amendment with the Eighteenth Amendment in 2010, which did not alter its text but reaffirmed its importance. The most notable change came with the Twenty-sixth Amendment in 2024, which specified a deadline for the complete elimination of riba by January 1, 2028. This amendment reflects a more assertive stance on the issue of interest, aligning with Islamic economic principles and addressing longstanding demands from various segments of society for a riba-free economy. The practical effect of this change is expected to influence the financial sector significantly, requiring adjustments in banking and financial practices to comply with the constitutional mandate.
