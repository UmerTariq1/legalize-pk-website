Article 190 of the Constitution of Pakistan, originally established in 1973, mandates that all executive and judicial authorities throughout the country must act in support of the Supreme Court. This article is crucial as it reinforces the authority of the Supreme Court, ensuring that its decisions and directives are implemented effectively across the nation. By obligating other branches of government to assist the Supreme Court, the article helps maintain the rule of law and the separation of powers, which are fundamental principles in a democratic system. It underscores the Supreme Court's role as the highest judicial authority, tasked with upholding the Constitution and ensuring justice.

In 2025, the Twenty-seventh Amendment introduced a significant change to Article 190 by expanding its scope to include a newly established Federal Constitutional Court alongside the Supreme Court. This amendment reflects a structural change in Pakistan's judicial system, likely aimed at addressing constitutional matters more efficiently by creating a specialized court. The practical effect of this change is that all executive and judicial authorities are now also required to support the Federal Constitutional Court, which suggests an intention to distribute the judicial workload and possibly enhance the focus on constitutional issues. The political context of this amendment, under the leadership of Asif Ali Zardari, may indicate an effort to strengthen judicial oversight and ensure that constitutional interpretations are handled by a dedicated body, thus potentially improving the legal framework's responsiveness to constitutional challenges.
