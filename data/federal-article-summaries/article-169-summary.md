Article 169 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, establishes the role and responsibilities of the Auditor-General. This article is crucial as it outlines the oversight function over the financial accounts of both the federal and provincial governments, as well as any authorities or bodies established by them. The Auditor-General is tasked with ensuring transparency and accountability in the management of public funds, which is a cornerstone of good governance and democracy. By auditing these accounts, the Auditor-General helps to prevent misuse of public resources and promotes fiscal responsibility within government institutions.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 169 has not been amended. This stability suggests that the foundational role and powers of the Auditor-General as initially conceived have remained relevant and effective over the years. The article grants the Auditor-General the authority to perform functions and exercise powers as determined by an Act of Parliament or, in the absence of such legislation, by an order of the President. This provision ensures that the Auditor-General's role can be adapted to changing circumstances through legislative or executive action, providing flexibility while maintaining the essential function of financial oversight. The unamended status of this article reflects a consensus on the importance of maintaining a robust mechanism for financial accountability in Pakistan's constitutional framework.
