Article 135 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the protocol for the temporary replacement of a Chief Minister in a province. This article is crucial for ensuring the continuity of governance at the provincial level in the event of a vacancy or the temporary unavailability of the Chief Minister. It outlines the procedure to be followed if the Chief Minister dies, resigns, or is otherwise unable to perform their duties. In such cases, the most senior Provincial Minister is called upon by the Governor to assume the responsibilities of the Chief Minister until a new one is elected or the current Chief Minister resumes their duties. The article also mandates that if the Provincial Assembly is not in session when the vacancy occurs, it must be convened within fourteen days to elect a new Chief Minister.

The significance of this article lies in its role in maintaining political stability and administrative continuity within a province. By clearly defining the chain of command and the process for electing a new Chief Minister, it prevents potential power vacuums and ensures that provincial governance can continue smoothly. The designation of the "most senior Provincial Minister" as the interim Chief Minister is determined by the Chief Minister, which provides a clear line of succession and minimizes confusion during transitions. This article has remained unchanged since its inception, indicating that its provisions have been deemed effective and sufficient for managing such transitions in provincial leadership.
