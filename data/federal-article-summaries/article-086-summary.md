Article 86 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the financial governance of the country during periods when the National Assembly is dissolved. Its primary purpose is to ensure that the Federal Government can continue to function and meet its financial obligations even in the absence of an active legislative body. Specifically, this article grants the Federal Government the power to authorize expenditure from the Federal Consolidated Fund for up to four months in any financial year. This provision is crucial because it allows the government to maintain operations and provide essential services without interruption, even when the National Assembly, which is responsible for approving the budget, is not in session.

The article is significant in maintaining the continuity of government operations and financial stability during transitional periods, such as when a new assembly is being elected or formed. It acts as a safeguard against potential financial paralysis that could occur if there were no mechanism to authorize government spending during these times. By allowing temporary financial autonomy to the executive branch, Article 86 ensures that the government can continue to meet its obligations, such as paying salaries, funding public services, and managing other necessary expenditures, until the new assembly is in place to review and approve the budget.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 86 has not been amended. This indicates that the provision has been deemed effective and necessary in its original form, reflecting a consensus on the importance of having a mechanism to manage government finances during periods when the legislative body is not operational. The stability of this article over time underscores its role as a fundamental component of Pakistan's constitutional framework, ensuring that governance and public administration can continue smoothly during political transitions.
