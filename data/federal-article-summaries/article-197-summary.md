Article 197 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the appointment of Additional Judges to the High Courts. This provision is crucial for maintaining the efficiency and functionality of the judiciary, particularly in situations where there is a vacancy, a judge is unable to perform their duties, or there is a need to temporarily increase the number of judges to handle an increased workload. The article empowers the President to appoint additional judges under these circumstances, following the procedure outlined in Article 193, which deals with the qualifications and appointment process for High Court judges.

The significance of Article 197 lies in its role in ensuring that the judicial system can adapt to changing circumstances and demands. By allowing for the appointment of additional judges, the article helps prevent delays in the administration of justice, which is a fundamental aspect of upholding the rule of law. The provision ensures that the High Courts can continue to function smoothly and efficiently, even when faced with temporary challenges such as vacancies or increased caseloads.

Since its inclusion in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 197 has not been amended, indicating that its framework has been deemed sufficient to address the needs it was designed to meet. The stability of this article suggests that the mechanism for appointing additional judges has effectively supported the judiciary's ability to manage its responsibilities without requiring further legislative intervention. This stability is essential for maintaining public confidence in the judicial system, as it demonstrates a consistent approach to addressing potential disruptions in the courts' operations.
