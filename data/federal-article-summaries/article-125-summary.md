Article 125 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the concept of "votes on account," which is a financial mechanism allowing a Provincial Assembly to authorize expenditure in advance for a portion of a financial year. This provision is crucial because it ensures that the government can continue to function smoothly even if the formal budgetary process is not yet complete. Specifically, it allows the Provincial Assembly to grant funds for up to three months while the detailed budget is being finalized and approved as per Articles 122 and 123. This mechanism is important for maintaining governmental operations and services without interruption, particularly in situations where there might be delays in the budget approval process.

The article has not been amended since its inclusion in the original 1973 Constitution, indicating that its provisions have remained relevant and effective in managing the financial operations of provincial governments. By allowing a temporary financial arrangement, Article 125 provides flexibility and continuity in governance, ensuring that essential services and functions are not halted due to procedural delays. This reflects a pragmatic approach to fiscal management within the constitutional framework, balancing the need for legislative oversight with the practicalities of governance.
