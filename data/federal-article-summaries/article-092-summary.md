Article 92 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 outlines the appointment and roles of Federal Ministers and Ministers of State. It establishes the Prime Minister's authority to appoint these ministers from among the members of Parliament, ensuring that the executive branch is closely tied to the legislative body. This article is significant as it delineates the structure and limitations of the federal cabinet, which is crucial for maintaining a balanced and representative government. The requirement for ministers to take an oath before the President underscores the formal and solemn nature of their responsibilities. Additionally, the article provides mechanisms for resignation or removal, ensuring that the Prime Minister retains control over the composition of the cabinet, which is essential for effective governance.

The article was amended by the Eighteenth Amendment in 2010, which introduced significant changes to the structure of the federal cabinet. The amendment added a provision that the total strength of the cabinet, including Ministers of State, should not exceed eleven percent of the total membership of the Parliament. This change aimed to limit the size of the cabinet, promoting efficiency and reducing the potential for excessive governmental expenditure. The amendment also specified that this change would take effect from the next general election after the amendment's enactment, allowing for a transition period. This adjustment reflects a broader effort to streamline government operations and ensure that the executive branch remains accountable and manageable.
