Article 123 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, deals with the authentication of the schedule of authorized expenditure at the provincial level. This article outlines the process by which the Chief Minister of a province must authenticate a schedule that specifies the financial grants made by the Provincial Assembly and the sums required to meet expenditures charged upon the Provincial Consolidated Fund. The importance of this article lies in its role in ensuring fiscal accountability and transparency in provincial financial management. By requiring the Chief Minister to authenticate the schedule, the article establishes a formal procedure for approving provincial expenditures, thereby preventing unauthorized spending.

The article specifies that the authenticated schedule must be laid before the Provincial Assembly, although it is not subject to discussion or vote. This provision ensures that while the Assembly is informed of the expenditure details, the process remains efficient and free from potential delays that could arise from debates or voting. The requirement that no expenditure from the Provincial Consolidated Fund is deemed authorized unless included in the authenticated schedule underscores the article's role in maintaining strict control over provincial finances. This mechanism is crucial for upholding the principles of responsible governance and financial discipline within the provincial governments of Pakistan.

Since its introduction in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 123 has not been amended. This stability suggests that the procedures it establishes have been deemed effective and necessary for the financial governance of the provinces. By providing a clear framework for the authentication of provincial expenditures, Article 123 plays a vital role in the broader constitutional structure, supporting the financial integrity and accountability of provincial administrations in Pakistan.
