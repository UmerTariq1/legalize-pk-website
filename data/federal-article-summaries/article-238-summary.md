Article 238 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, establishes the procedure for amending the Constitution. This article is crucial as it outlines the process through which changes can be made to the country's supreme legal document. The ability to amend the Constitution is significant because it allows the legal framework to evolve and adapt to changing political, social, and economic circumstances. It ensures that the Constitution remains relevant and responsive to the needs of the nation while maintaining a balance between stability and flexibility.

The original text of Article 238, as introduced in 1973, states that the Constitution may be amended by an Act of Parliament. This provision underscores the role of the legislative branch in constitutional amendments, emphasizing the importance of parliamentary approval in making any changes to the foundational legal structure of the country. The requirement for parliamentary involvement ensures that amendments are subject to democratic processes and reflect the will of the elected representatives of the people.

Since its inception, Article 238 has not been amended, indicating that the original mechanism for constitutional amendments has remained intact. This stability suggests that the process established in 1973 has been deemed adequate for addressing the needs of the nation over the years. The unaltered state of this article highlights its foundational role in maintaining the integrity and continuity of the constitutional framework in Pakistan.
