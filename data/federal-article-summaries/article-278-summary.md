Article 278 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 addresses the role of the Auditor-General concerning accounts that were not completed or audited before the Constitution came into effect. This provision ensures that the Auditor-General has the authority to audit these pending accounts with the same powers and responsibilities as they would for any other accounts under the Constitution. The inclusion of this article was crucial for maintaining financial oversight and accountability during the transition to the new constitutional framework established in 1973. By granting the Auditor-General the ability to audit these pre-existing accounts, the article aimed to ensure continuity and integrity in the financial administration of the state.

The article has not been amended since its inclusion in the original 1973 Constitution, indicating that its purpose and function have remained relevant and uncontested over the years. Its presence underscores the importance of financial accountability and transparency in governance, ensuring that no financial matters are left unchecked due to the transition to a new constitutional order. This continuity helps in maintaining public trust in the financial management of the state and ensures that all financial activities, past and present, are subject to scrutiny and oversight.
