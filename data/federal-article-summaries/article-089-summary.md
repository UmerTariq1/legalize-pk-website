Article 89 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 grants the President the power to issue ordinances when the Parliament is not in session. This power is significant because it allows the executive branch to enact temporary laws without the immediate approval of the legislative branch, addressing urgent situations that require immediate legal intervention. The ordinances issued under this article have the same effect as laws passed by Parliament but are temporary and subject to specific conditions and limitations. This mechanism ensures that the government can respond swiftly to emergencies or unforeseen circumstances while maintaining a balance of power by requiring subsequent legislative approval.

The original text of Article 89, as established in 1973, allowed the President to issue ordinances when the National Assembly was not in session. These ordinances were to be laid before the National Assembly or both Houses, depending on the subject matter, and would automatically expire after four months unless disapproved earlier by a resolution. The Eighteenth Amendment, enacted in 2010, introduced significant changes to this article. It expanded the conditions under which the President could issue ordinances to include times when either the Senate or the National Assembly was not in session. Additionally, the amendment extended the period for which an ordinance could remain in effect from four months to 120 days, with the possibility of a one-time extension for another 120 days, subject to legislative approval.

These changes reflect a shift towards greater legislative oversight and a more structured process for extending ordinances, thereby enhancing the checks and balances within the government. By allowing only a single extension, the amendment aims to prevent the executive from bypassing the legislative process indefinitely. This evolution of Article 89 underscores the ongoing efforts to refine the balance of power between the executive and legislative branches in Pakistan's constitutional framework, ensuring that the President's ordinance-making power is exercised responsibly and with appropriate legislative scrutiny.
