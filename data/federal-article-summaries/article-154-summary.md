Article 154 of the Constitution of Pakistan establishes the framework for the functioning and procedural rules of a Council responsible for formulating and regulating policies related to matters listed in Part II of the Federal Legislative List. Initially, this article also included oversight over electricity matters in the Concurrent Legislative List, reflecting the shared responsibilities between federal and provincial governments. The Council's decisions are made by majority opinion, and it has the authority to create its own procedural rules until Parliament legislates otherwise. This article is significant as it outlines the collaborative governance structure between federal and provincial entities, ensuring that policies are formulated with input from multiple levels of government.

The Eighteenth Amendment in 2010 brought significant changes to Article 154. It removed the Council's oversight over electricity matters, reflecting a shift in legislative priorities or a reallocation of responsibilities. The amendment also introduced a requirement for the Council to be constituted within thirty days of the Prime Minister taking office, ensuring timely establishment and operation. Additionally, the amendment mandated that the Council have a permanent Secretariat and meet at least once every ninety days, with provisions for urgent meetings at a province's request. These changes aimed to enhance the Council's efficiency and responsiveness, ensuring regular and structured interactions between federal and provincial governments. The amendment also reordered the article's clauses, emphasizing the procedural aspects and the binding nature of parliamentary directions to the Council, thereby reinforcing the Council's accountability to Parliament.
