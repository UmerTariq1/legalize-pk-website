Article 64 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the circumstances under which a seat in Parliament may become vacant. This article is crucial as it outlines the procedural aspects for maintaining the integrity and functionality of the legislative body. It establishes two primary scenarios for vacating a seat: voluntary resignation and prolonged absenteeism. A member of Parliament can resign by submitting a written notice to the Speaker or Chairman, depending on whether they belong to the National Assembly or the Senate. This provision ensures that members who no longer wish to serve can formally and clearly relinquish their responsibilities, allowing for a smooth transition and the potential for a by-election to fill the vacancy.

Additionally, the article provides a mechanism for addressing absenteeism. If a member is absent for forty consecutive days without the House's permission, the House has the authority to declare their seat vacant. This rule is significant as it ensures that members remain committed to their legislative duties and that the parliamentary process is not hindered by prolonged absences. By setting a clear threshold for absenteeism, the article helps maintain the accountability and efficiency of the legislative process.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 64 has not been amended, indicating that its provisions have been deemed effective and appropriate for managing parliamentary vacancies. The article plays a vital role in upholding the democratic process by ensuring that parliamentary seats are occupied by active and willing participants, thereby supporting the overall governance and legislative function in Pakistan.
