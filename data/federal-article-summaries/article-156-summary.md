Article 156 of the Constitution of Pakistan establishes the National Economic Council (NEC), a key institution tasked with overseeing and advising on the economic policies of the country. Initially, as per the 1973 Constitution, the NEC was to be constituted by the President and chaired by the Prime Minister. It included members nominated by the President, with at least one member from each province recommended by the provincial governments. The NEC's primary role was to review the country's economic condition and formulate plans for financial, commercial, social, and economic policies, guided by the Principles of Policy outlined in the Constitution.

The Eighteenth Amendment in 2010 brought significant changes to Article 156, reflecting a shift towards greater provincial autonomy and a more structured approach to economic governance. The amendment redefined the composition of the NEC, making the Chief Ministers of the provinces and a member nominated by each Chief Minister part of the council, along with four additional members nominated by the Prime Minister. This change aimed to ensure that provincial interests were directly represented in national economic planning. Furthermore, the amendment introduced procedural requirements, such as mandating that the NEC meet at least twice a year and submit an annual report to Parliament, thereby increasing transparency and accountability. The NEC was also required to be constituted within thirty days of the Prime Minister taking office, ensuring timely establishment and operation. These changes were part of broader efforts to decentralize power and enhance the role of provinces in the federal structure, reflecting the political climate and demands for provincial representation in national decision-making processes.
