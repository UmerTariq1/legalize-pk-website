Article 15 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, is concerned with the right to freedom of movement for citizens within the country. This article establishes that every citizen has the right to stay in, enter, and move freely throughout Pakistan, as well as to reside and settle in any part of the country. However, this right is not absolute and can be subject to reasonable restrictions imposed by law in the public interest. The inclusion of this article underscores the importance of personal liberty and mobility, which are essential for the exercise of other fundamental rights and for the economic and social development of individuals and communities.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 15 has not been amended. This stability suggests a broad consensus on the importance of freedom of movement as a fundamental right. The provision allows for restrictions, but these must be reasonable and justified by public interest, which provides a balance between individual freedoms and the state's responsibility to maintain order and security. The article plays a crucial role in ensuring that citizens can pursue opportunities and live in areas of their choosing, contributing to the unity and integration of the nation by allowing people to move and interact freely across provincial and regional boundaries.
