Article 9 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, is a fundamental right that guarantees the security of person. It establishes that no individual can be deprived of their life or personal freedom unless it is done according to the law. This article is crucial as it forms the bedrock of personal liberty and protection against arbitrary actions by the state. It ensures that any deprivation of life or liberty must be justified by legal procedures, thereby safeguarding individuals from unlawful detention or punishment. This provision is a cornerstone of democratic societies, emphasizing the rule of law and the protection of human rights.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 9 has not been amended. This indicates the enduring importance and acceptance of the principle it enshrines. The unaltered state of this article suggests a broad consensus on the necessity of protecting personal security and liberty as fundamental rights. It serves as a constant reminder to the state and its institutions of their obligation to uphold the law and protect citizens' rights, reinforcing the legal framework that prevents misuse of power and ensures justice.
