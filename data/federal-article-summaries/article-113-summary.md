Article 113 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, outlines the qualifications necessary for an individual to be elected as a member of a Provincial Assembly. This article is crucial as it sets the baseline criteria for who can participate in the legislative process at the provincial level, ensuring that representatives meet certain standards of citizenship, age, and voter registration. Specifically, it requires that a candidate must be a citizen of Pakistan, at least twenty-five years old, and registered as a voter in any electoral roll for the Assembly election. Additionally, the article allows for further qualifications to be prescribed by an Act of Parliament, providing flexibility to adapt to changing political and social contexts.

Since its inception, Article 113 has remained unchanged, indicating a consistent approach to the basic qualifications for provincial legislators. This stability suggests that the original framers of the Constitution, under the leadership of Zulfikar Ali Bhutto, established criteria that have been deemed appropriate and sufficient over the decades. The article's persistence without amendment reflects a consensus on the fundamental requirements for provincial legislative candidates, emphasizing the importance of citizenship, maturity, and electoral participation in the democratic process of Pakistan. By maintaining these standards, the article helps ensure that those who are elected to provincial assemblies are adequately prepared to represent their constituents and contribute to the governance of their provinces.
