Article 175-L, introduced through the Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025, establishes the seat of the Federal Constitutional Court in Pakistan. This article specifies that the permanent location of this court is in Islamabad, the capital city of Pakistan. However, it also provides flexibility by allowing the court to convene in other locations as deemed necessary by the Chief Justice of the Federal Constitutional Court, with the President's prior approval. This flexibility ensures that the court can be more accessible and responsive to different regions of the country, potentially enhancing the efficiency and reach of constitutional justice.

The creation of the Federal Constitutional Court and the specification of its seat are significant as they underscore the importance of a dedicated judicial body to interpret and uphold the Constitution of Pakistan. By having a permanent seat in Islamabad, the court is positioned at the heart of the country's political and administrative activities, symbolizing its central role in maintaining constitutional governance. The ability to sit in other locations reflects a pragmatic approach to justice, allowing the court to address constitutional matters across the nation without being geographically constrained.

The introduction of this article through the Twenty-seventh Amendment during Asif Ali Zardari's political tenure may reflect a strategic move to strengthen the constitutional framework and ensure that constitutional issues are addressed by a specialized court. This development could be seen as a response to the evolving legal and political landscape in Pakistan, where the need for a robust mechanism to interpret constitutional matters has become increasingly apparent. The establishment of the Federal Constitutional Court is a pivotal step in reinforcing the rule of law and ensuring that constitutional rights and principles are upheld across the country.
