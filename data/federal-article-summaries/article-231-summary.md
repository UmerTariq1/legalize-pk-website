Article 231 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 establishes the framework for the procedural rules governing the Islamic Council, a body tasked with advising on Islamic law and ensuring that laws conform to Islamic principles. The article specifies that the Council itself is responsible for creating its rules of procedure, but these rules require the approval of the President. This provision underscores the importance of the Islamic Council in the legislative process and ensures that its operations are conducted in an orderly and consistent manner, reflecting the Council's significant role in aligning national laws with Islamic teachings.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 231 has not undergone any amendments. This stability suggests that the original framework for the Council's procedural rules has been deemed effective and appropriate by successive governments. The requirement for presidential approval of the Council's rules indicates a balance between the Council's autonomy and executive oversight, ensuring that the procedures align with broader governmental policies and priorities. This article is crucial as it facilitates the Council's function in the legislative process, impacting how laws are reviewed and potentially influencing legislative outcomes to reflect Islamic values.
