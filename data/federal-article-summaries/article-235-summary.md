Article 235 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the scenario of a financial emergency. It empowers the President to declare such an emergency if he believes that the economic life, financial stability, or credit of Pakistan, or any part of it, is under threat. This declaration is made after consulting with the provincial governors. The significance of this article lies in its provision for federal intervention in provincial financial matters during times of economic distress. It allows the federal government to issue directives to provinces to maintain financial propriety and take necessary measures to stabilize the economy.

The article also grants the President the authority to mandate reductions in salaries and allowances of provincial employees, and similarly, federal employees, during the period of financial emergency. This power underscores the central government's ability to enforce austerity measures to manage financial crises effectively. The inclusion of this article in the original constitution reflects the foresight of the framers in anticipating potential economic challenges and providing a constitutional mechanism to address them.

Since its inception, Article 235 has not been amended, indicating that its provisions have remained relevant and sufficient for addressing financial emergencies. The article's unaltered status suggests that the balance it strikes between federal authority and provincial autonomy has been deemed appropriate by successive governments. Its existence is crucial for maintaining economic stability and ensuring that the federal government can act decisively in times of financial distress, thereby safeguarding the nation's economic interests.
