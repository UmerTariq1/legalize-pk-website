Article 175-F, introduced through the Twenty-seventh Amendment in November 2025, establishes the appellate jurisdiction of the Federal Constitutional Court in Pakistan. This article outlines the conditions under which this court can hear appeals from decisions made by High Courts. Specifically, it allows the Federal Constitutional Court to hear appeals if an Act of Parliament provides for it, if a High Court certifies that a case involves a significant constitutional question, or if the Federal Constitutional Court itself grants permission to appeal a High Court's decision under Article 199. However, it explicitly excludes appeals related to family and rent matters from being heard by the Federal Constitutional Court.

The creation of this article marks a significant shift in the judicial structure of Pakistan, as it transfers certain appellate responsibilities from the Supreme Court to the newly established Federal Constitutional Court. This change aims to streamline the judicial process by creating a specialized court to handle constitutional matters, potentially leading to more focused and efficient adjudication of such cases. The amendment also includes a transitional provision, transferring all relevant pending cases from the Supreme Court to the Federal Constitutional Court, ensuring continuity and consistency in the handling of these appeals. This development reflects an effort to enhance the constitutional framework by providing a dedicated forum for constitutional interpretation and disputes, which could have far-reaching implications for the legal landscape in Pakistan.
