Article 257 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 addresses the potential accession of the State of Jammu and Kashmir to Pakistan. This provision is significant as it outlines the framework for how Pakistan would establish its relationship with Jammu and Kashmir if the people of that region choose to join Pakistan. The article emphasizes the importance of the will of the people of Jammu and Kashmir in determining the nature of their relationship with Pakistan. This reflects Pakistan's stance on the Kashmir issue, highlighting the principle of self-determination for the people of Jammu and Kashmir.

Since its inclusion in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 257 has not been amended. Its unchanged status underscores the consistent position of Pakistan regarding the Kashmir conflict, which has been a central issue in the region's geopolitics. The article's focus on the wishes of the people of Jammu and Kashmir suggests a commitment to a democratic process in resolving the matter, aligning with international calls for a peaceful and negotiated settlement. The provision is crucial as it sets a constitutional basis for any future political developments concerning Jammu and Kashmir's status vis-à-vis Pakistan.
