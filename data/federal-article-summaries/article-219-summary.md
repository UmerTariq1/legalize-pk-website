Article 219 of the Constitution of Pakistan outlines the duties of the Election Commission, which is a crucial institution responsible for ensuring free and fair elections in the country. Initially, in the 1973 Constitution, the article assigned these duties to the Election Commissioner, focusing on preparing and revising electoral rolls, organizing elections to the Senate and Provincial Assemblies, and appointing Election Tribunals. This framework was essential for maintaining the democratic process by ensuring that elections were conducted in an orderly and transparent manner.

Over the years, Article 219 has undergone several amendments to enhance the electoral process's robustness and adaptability. The Eighteenth Amendment in 2010 marked a significant shift by transferring responsibilities from the Commissioner to the Commission, reflecting a move towards a more collective and institutional approach. This amendment expanded the Commission's duties to include holding general elections for the National Assembly, Provincial Assemblies, and local governments, as well as any other functions specified by Parliament. The Twentieth Amendment in 2012 further clarified the transitional arrangements for the Commission's responsibilities until new members were appointed, ensuring continuity in electoral oversight.

The Twenty-second Amendment in 2016 refined the Commission's duties by specifying that electoral rolls should be prepared and periodically updated not only for national and provincial elections but also for local governments. This change underscored the importance of keeping electoral data current and accurate, thereby strengthening the integrity of the electoral process. These amendments collectively reflect Pakistan's evolving democratic landscape, aiming to enhance the efficiency, transparency, and inclusivity of its electoral system.
