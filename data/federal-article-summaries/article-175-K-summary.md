Article 175-K, introduced through the Twenty-seventh Amendment in November 2025, establishes the provision for appointing acting judges to the Federal Constitutional Court of Pakistan. This article is crucial as it ensures the continuous and efficient functioning of the Federal Constitutional Court, which plays a vital role in interpreting the Constitution and adjudicating constitutional matters. The article allows the President of Pakistan to appoint a judge from either the Supreme Court or a High Court to temporarily fill a vacancy or cover for a judge who is absent or unable to perform their duties. This appointment can include retired judges, thereby broadening the pool of eligible candidates and ensuring that the court can maintain its operations without interruption.

The introduction of this article reflects a practical approach to addressing potential gaps in the judiciary's functioning due to vacancies or absences. By allowing temporary appointments, the amendment aims to prevent delays in judicial proceedings and maintain the court's ability to uphold constitutional governance. The provision for acting judges underscores the importance of flexibility and adaptability in the judicial system, ensuring that the Federal Constitutional Court can continue to fulfill its critical role in the constitutional framework of Pakistan without being hindered by personnel shortages. This change likely arose from a need to enhance the judiciary's efficiency and responsiveness, reflecting a broader commitment to strengthening the rule of law in the country.
