Article 186-A of the Constitution of Pakistan establishes the authority to transfer legal cases between High Courts or to the Supreme Court. Initially introduced through the Twenty-sixth Amendment in October 2024, this article granted the Supreme Court the power to transfer any case, appeal, or proceeding from one High Court to another or to itself if deemed necessary for justice. This provision is significant as it ensures that cases can be moved to courts that may be better suited to handle them, potentially leading to more efficient and fair outcomes. It reflects the importance of flexibility within the judicial system to address issues of jurisdiction and to ensure that justice is served effectively.

The article underwent a significant change with the Twenty-seventh Amendment in November 2025. This amendment expanded the authority to include the Federal Constitutional Court alongside the Supreme Court. The change implies the establishment or recognition of a Federal Constitutional Court, which now shares the power to transfer cases, provided it has the jurisdiction to hear them. This amendment likely reflects a broader restructuring or enhancement of the judicial system, possibly aimed at addressing a growing number of constitutional matters or improving the efficiency of case management across the country's courts. The practical effect of this change is an increased capacity for handling complex legal issues and a more versatile judicial framework, which can adapt to the evolving needs of the legal landscape in Pakistan.
