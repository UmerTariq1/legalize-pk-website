Article 188 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 grants the Supreme Court the authority to review its own judgments and orders. This power is significant as it allows the highest court in the country to reassess its decisions, ensuring that justice is served and any errors or oversights can be corrected. The ability to review decisions is a critical aspect of judicial accountability and integrity, providing a mechanism for the court to maintain consistency and fairness in its rulings. This article underscores the importance of flexibility within the judicial process, allowing for the correction of potential mistakes that may have occurred in the court's initial judgment.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 188 has not been amended. This stability suggests a consensus on the importance of the Supreme Court's ability to review its decisions, reflecting a foundational principle in the judicial system of Pakistan. The article's unaltered state indicates that the provision has been deemed effective and necessary for the proper functioning of the judiciary, without requiring further legislative or procedural adjustments. The Supreme Court's review power is subject to any relevant Acts of Parliament and rules established by the court itself, ensuring that the process is conducted within a structured legal framework. This balance between judicial independence and legislative oversight is crucial for maintaining the rule of law and public confidence in the judicial system.
