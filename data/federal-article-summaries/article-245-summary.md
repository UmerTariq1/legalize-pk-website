Article 245 of the Constitution of Pakistan outlines the functions of the Armed Forces, emphasizing their role in defending the country against external threats and assisting civil authorities when required. Initially, the article established that the Armed Forces operate under the direction of the Federal Government, ensuring a clear chain of command and accountability. This provision is crucial as it delineates the military's responsibilities, ensuring that their primary focus remains on national defense and support to civil power, thereby maintaining a balance between military and civilian governance.

The Seventh Amendment in 1977 introduced significant changes to Article 245, expanding its scope and implications. It added clauses that effectively insulated the Armed Forces' actions, when acting in aid of civil power, from judicial scrutiny. Specifically, it stated that any directions given by the Federal Government to the Armed Forces could not be questioned in court, and it restricted the jurisdiction of High Courts over areas where the military was operating in support of civil authorities. This amendment was likely influenced by the political climate of the time, which saw increased military involvement in civil matters, and aimed to provide legal cover for such actions. The practical effect of these changes was a reduction in judicial oversight over military operations within the country, potentially impacting the balance of power between the judiciary and the executive, and affecting citizens' ability to seek legal recourse in situations involving military intervention in civil affairs.
