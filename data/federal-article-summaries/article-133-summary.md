Article 133 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the continuity of the Chief Minister's office. It ensures that the Chief Minister remains in their position until a successor officially takes over. This provision is crucial for maintaining governmental stability and continuity, especially during transitions of power. The article also clarifies that the dissolution of the Provincial Assembly does not disqualify the Chief Minister or any Provincial Minister from continuing in office. This means that even if the assembly is dissolved, the executive functions of the province can continue without interruption, preventing a power vacuum and ensuring that governance and administrative duties are not halted.

The article has not been amended since its inception, indicating that its provisions have been deemed adequate and effective in maintaining the intended continuity of governance. By allowing the Chief Minister to remain in office until a successor is appointed, the article provides a safeguard against potential political instability that could arise during periods of governmental transition. This continuity is vital for the smooth functioning of provincial administrations and for upholding the rule of law during times when the legislative body is not in session.
