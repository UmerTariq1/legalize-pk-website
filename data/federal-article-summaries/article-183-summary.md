Article 183 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the location of the Supreme Court, which is the highest judicial authority in the country. The article initially established that the permanent seat of the Supreme Court would be in Islamabad, the capital city. However, it also allowed for flexibility by permitting the Supreme Court to hold sessions in other locations as determined by the Chief Justice of Pakistan, with the President's approval. This provision was crucial in ensuring that the Supreme Court could operate effectively and accessibly across the country, reflecting the need for judicial processes to be adaptable to the geographical and logistical realities of Pakistan.

The article underwent a significant change with the Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025. The amendment made a subtle yet important alteration by changing the reference from "Chief Justice of Pakistan" to "Chief Justice of Supreme Court." While this might seem like a minor linguistic adjustment, it could reflect an effort to standardize terminology or clarify roles within the judiciary. The practical effect of this change on citizens or institutions is likely minimal, as the core function and authority of the Chief Justice in determining the locations for Supreme Court sittings remain unchanged. However, it underscores the ongoing process of refining constitutional language to ensure clarity and precision in legal texts.
