Article 83 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, deals with the authentication of the schedule of authorized expenditure. This article outlines the process by which the Prime Minister must authenticate a schedule that specifies the financial grants made by the National Assembly and the sums required to meet expenditures charged upon the Federal Consolidated Fund. The Federal Consolidated Fund is essentially the government's main bank account, where all revenues and loans are deposited and from which all government expenditures are made. The purpose of this article is to ensure that there is a formal and transparent process for approving government spending, which is crucial for maintaining fiscal discipline and accountability in the management of public funds.

The article mandates that the Prime Minister's signature is necessary to authenticate the schedule of authorized expenditures. This schedule includes the grants approved by the National Assembly and the necessary expenditures that do not exceed the amounts previously presented to the Assembly. Once authenticated, this schedule must be laid before the National Assembly, although it is not subject to further discussion or voting. This provision underscores the importance of executive accountability while also streamlining the process to prevent unnecessary delays in government spending.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 83 has not been amended, indicating that the original framework for authenticating government expenditure has remained consistent. This stability suggests that the process established by this article has been effective in balancing the need for executive oversight with the practical requirements of government financial operations. By ensuring that all expenditures are properly authorized and documented, Article 83 plays a vital role in the constitutional structure of Pakistan, supporting the principles of transparency and accountability in public finance.
