Article 129 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 outlines the structure and responsibilities of the provincial government. It establishes that the executive authority of a province is exercised in the name of the Governor by the Provincial Government, which includes the Chief Minister and Provincial Ministers. The Chief Minister is the central figure in this setup, acting as the head of the provincial executive and responsible for implementing policies and decisions. The article also emphasizes the collective responsibility of the Chief Minister and Provincial Ministers to the Provincial Assembly, ensuring that the provincial executive remains accountable to the elected representatives of the people.

The article was originally part of the 1973 Constitution and remained unchanged until the Eighteenth Amendment in 2010. This amendment did not alter the substantive provisions of Article 129 but updated the metadata to reflect the amendment's enactment. The Eighteenth Amendment was a significant constitutional reform aimed at enhancing provincial autonomy and decentralizing power from the federal government to the provinces. Although Article 129 itself was not substantively changed, the broader context of the amendment strengthened the role and independence of provincial governments, thereby reinforcing the principles of federalism in Pakistan's constitutional framework. This shift was part of a broader political movement to address longstanding demands for greater provincial autonomy and to rectify the imbalance of power between the federal and provincial governments.
