Article 20 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, is a fundamental provision that guarantees the freedom of religion to the citizens of Pakistan. It establishes the right for every individual to profess, practice, and propagate their religion, ensuring that religious freedom is a protected right within the country. Additionally, it grants every religious group and sect the autonomy to establish, maintain, and manage their religious institutions. This article is significant as it underscores the commitment of the state to uphold religious freedom and pluralism, which are essential for a diverse society like Pakistan. It ensures that individuals and communities can freely express their religious beliefs and manage their religious affairs without undue interference, subject to considerations of law, public order, and morality.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 20 has not been amended. This consistency reflects the enduring importance of religious freedom in Pakistan's constitutional framework. The lack of amendments suggests a broad consensus on the necessity of these rights and the role they play in maintaining social harmony and respecting the diverse religious landscape of the country. By safeguarding these freedoms, the article contributes to the protection of minority rights and the promotion of tolerance, which are crucial for the peaceful coexistence of different religious communities in Pakistan.
