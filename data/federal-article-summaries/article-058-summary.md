Article 58 of the Constitution of Pakistan deals with the dissolution of the National Assembly, a critical aspect of the country's parliamentary system. Initially, the article empowered the President to dissolve the National Assembly upon the advice of the Prime Minister. This provision ensures that the executive branch, led by the Prime Minister, maintains a significant influence over the legislative branch, allowing for the dissolution of the assembly if deemed necessary. This mechanism is crucial for maintaining political stability and ensuring that the government can function effectively, especially in situations where the assembly may be unable to perform its duties.

Over the years, Article 58 has undergone several amendments, reflecting the shifting balance of power between the President and the Prime Minister. The Eighth Amendment in 1985, introduced during General Zia-ul-Haq's regime, expanded the President's powers, allowing him to dissolve the National Assembly at his discretion under certain conditions, such as when no member could command a majority or when the government could not function according to the Constitution. This change significantly increased the President's authority, often criticized as undermining the parliamentary system by enabling potential misuse of power for political purposes.

The Thirteenth Amendment in 1997, under Prime Minister Nawaz Sharif, curtailed these discretionary powers, removing the President's ability to dissolve the assembly without the Prime Minister's advice. However, the Seventeenth Amendment in 2003, during Pervez Musharraf's presidency, reinstated some of these powers, albeit with a requirement for judicial review by the Supreme Court. Finally, the Eighteenth Amendment in 2010, under President Asif Ali Zardari, again limited the President's discretionary powers, emphasizing the role of the Prime Minister in the dissolution process. These changes reflect ongoing tensions and negotiations over executive power in Pakistan's political landscape, aiming to balance authority between the President and the Prime Minister while safeguarding democratic processes.
