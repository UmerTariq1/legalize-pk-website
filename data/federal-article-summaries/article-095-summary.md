Article 95 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the protocol for temporarily filling the office of the Prime Minister in specific circumstances. This article is crucial as it ensures the continuity of executive functions in the event of a vacancy or temporary absence in the Prime Minister's office. It outlines that if the Prime Minister dies or the office becomes vacant, the most senior Federal Minister, as designated by the Prime Minister, is called upon by the President to perform the duties of the Prime Minister. This provision ensures that there is no power vacuum and that the executive branch continues to function smoothly until a new Prime Minister is elected.

The article also specifies the procedure for electing a new Prime Minister. If the National Assembly is in session when the vacancy occurs, it must immediately proceed to elect a new Prime Minister. If the Assembly is not in session, the President is required to summon it within fourteen days to elect a new leader. Additionally, the article provides for situations where the Prime Minister is temporarily unable to perform his duties due to absence from the country or other reasons. In such cases, the most senior Federal Minister steps in to fulfill the Prime Minister's responsibilities until he returns or resumes his duties.

This article has not been amended since its inclusion in the original 1973 Constitution, indicating its foundational role in maintaining governmental stability during transitions. By clearly defining the line of succession and the process for electing a new Prime Minister, Article 95 plays a vital role in safeguarding the continuity and stability of Pakistan's executive leadership.
