Article 138 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 is concerned with the delegation of functions within the provincial framework. It allows the Provincial Assembly, upon the recommendation of the Provincial Government, to legally assign responsibilities to officers or authorities that are subordinate to the Provincial Government. This article is significant because it provides a mechanism for decentralizing administrative functions within a province, enabling more localized governance and potentially more efficient administration. By allowing the Provincial Assembly to confer functions on subordinate authorities, the article supports the principle of subsidiarity, where decisions are made as closely as possible to the citizens they affect.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 138 has not been amended. This stability suggests that the provision has been effective in its purpose or has not been contentious enough to warrant changes. The ability to delegate functions is crucial in a federal system like Pakistan's, where provincial autonomy and the efficient administration of provincial affairs are essential for addressing the diverse needs of the country's regions. By empowering provincial authorities to delegate tasks, the article helps ensure that governance can be responsive and adaptable to local conditions, which is vital in a country with significant regional diversity.
