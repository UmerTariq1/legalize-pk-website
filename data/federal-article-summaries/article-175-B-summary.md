Article 175-B of the Constitution of Pakistan, introduced through the Twenty-seventh Amendment in November 2025, establishes the Federal Constitutional Court. This court is designed to serve as a specialized judicial body with a focus on constitutional matters, ensuring that the interpretation and application of the Constitution are handled by a dedicated institution. The creation of this court underscores the importance of having a distinct judicial entity to address constitutional disputes, which can be complex and require specialized legal expertise. The court is to be composed of a Chief Justice and other judges, with the number of judges being determined by the Parliament or, in the absence of such determination, by the President. Importantly, the amendment ensures provincial representation by mandating an equal number of judges from each province, promoting a sense of balance and fairness in the court's composition.

The establishment of the Federal Constitutional Court reflects a significant evolution in Pakistan's judicial framework, aimed at strengthening the constitutional governance of the country. By having a court specifically dedicated to constitutional issues, the amendment seeks to enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of judicial review processes. This development may also help in reducing the burden on the Supreme Court, allowing it to focus more on other judicial matters. The President is empowered to make necessary provisions for the court's establishment and functioning, ensuring that the court can operate smoothly and fulfill its intended role. This amendment, introduced during Asif Ali Zardari's tenure, highlights a political commitment to refining the judicial system and ensuring that constitutional matters receive the focused attention they require.
