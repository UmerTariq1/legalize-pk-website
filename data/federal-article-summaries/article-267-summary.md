Article 267 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, is concerned with granting the President the authority to address and resolve any difficulties that may arise in the implementation of the Constitution. This provision was included to ensure a smooth transition and effective operation of the new constitutional framework established in 1973. Specifically, it allows the President to make necessary modifications, additions, or omissions to the Constitution's provisions within a specified period, either before the Constitution's commencement or within three months thereafter. This power is intended to facilitate the practical application of the Constitution and to address unforeseen issues that could impede its implementation.

The article also includes a mechanism for legislative oversight. Any order made by the President under this article must be presented to both Houses of Parliament without undue delay. The order remains in effect unless both Houses pass a resolution disapproving it. In the event of disagreement between the two Houses, the order continues until a resolution is passed in a joint sitting. This oversight ensures that while the President has the power to make necessary adjustments, there is a check on this power through parliamentary review, maintaining a balance between executive action and legislative authority.

Article 267 has not been amended since its inclusion in the original Constitution of 1973. Its significance lies in its role as a transitional provision, designed to ensure that the new constitutional order could be implemented effectively and without unnecessary hindrance. By allowing temporary adjustments, it provided flexibility in the early days of the Constitution's operation, reflecting the pragmatic need to adapt to real-world challenges while establishing a new legal and political framework.
