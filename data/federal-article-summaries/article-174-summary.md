Article 174 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 establishes the legal framework for how the Federation and the Provinces can engage in legal proceedings. Specifically, it states that the Federation can initiate or be subject to lawsuits under the name of "Pakistan," while each Province can do so under its respective name. This article is crucial because it provides a clear legal identity to both the federal and provincial governments, allowing them to participate in legal actions as distinct entities. This is important for maintaining the rule of law and ensuring that governmental bodies can be held accountable in courts, which is a fundamental aspect of a functioning democracy.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 174 has not been amended. This suggests that the provision has been deemed effective and sufficient in its original form to address the needs of legal proceedings involving the state and its provinces. The lack of amendments also indicates a consensus on the clarity and necessity of this article's stipulations, reflecting its foundational role in the legal and constitutional framework of Pakistan. By providing a mechanism for legal accountability, Article 174 supports the broader constitutional structure by ensuring that both federal and provincial governments can be challenged and held responsible in legal settings, thereby reinforcing the principles of justice and governance.
