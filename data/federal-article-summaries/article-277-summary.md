Article 277 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973, titled "Transitional financial provisions," was established to manage the financial transition following the adoption of the new constitution. This article was crucial in ensuring that the financial operations of the government continued smoothly during the transition period. It allowed the schedule of authorized expenditure, which had been authenticated by the President for the financial year ending June 30, 1974, to remain valid for that year. This provision was necessary to maintain continuity in government spending and operations without interruption due to the constitutional change.

Additionally, the article empowered the President to authorize the withdrawal of funds from the Federal Consolidated Fund for any financial year before July 1, 1973, if the expenditure exceeded the authorized amount for that year. This was an important measure to address any financial discrepancies or needs that arose during the transition. The article also extended similar provisions to the provinces, with the Governor of each province taking on the role equivalent to the President, and the Provincial Consolidated Fund being used instead of the Federal Consolidated Fund. This ensured that both federal and provincial governments could manage their finances effectively during the transitional period.

The article has not been amended since its inception, indicating that its purpose was specific to the transitional phase following the establishment of the 1973 Constitution. Its role was primarily to provide a legal framework for financial management during a time of significant political and administrative change, ensuring stability and continuity in government functions.
