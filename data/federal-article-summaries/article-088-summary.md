Article 88 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, establishes the framework for the financial governance of the National Assembly and the Senate. It is designed to ensure that the expenditure of these legislative bodies is managed within the limits of authorized appropriations. This is achieved by placing the control of such expenditures under the purview of the respective Finance Committees of the National Assembly and the Senate. The article is significant because it provides a structured approach to financial oversight within the legislative branch, ensuring that public funds are used responsibly and in accordance with the budgetary allocations approved by the legislature.

The composition of the Finance Committees is clearly defined in the article. Each committee includes the Speaker or Chairman of the respective house, the Minister of Finance, and other members elected by the National Assembly or the Senate. This composition ensures that there is a balance of power and representation in financial decision-making, combining political leadership with financial expertise. Additionally, the article grants these committees the authority to establish their own procedural rules, allowing for flexibility and adaptability in their operations.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 88 has not been amended. This stability suggests that the framework it provides has been effective in managing the financial affairs of Pakistan's legislative bodies. By entrusting financial oversight to committees that include both political and financial leaders, the article helps maintain transparency and accountability in the use of public funds, which is crucial for maintaining public trust in governmental institutions.
