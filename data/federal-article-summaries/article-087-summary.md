Article 87 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, establishes the framework for the secretariats of the Parliament, which includes both the National Assembly and the Senate. The article mandates that each House of Parliament should have its own separate secretariat. This separation is crucial for maintaining the independence and efficient functioning of each House, allowing them to manage their administrative affairs independently. However, the article also provides flexibility by allowing for the creation of posts that can be shared between the two Houses, ensuring that resources can be utilized efficiently where necessary.

Furthermore, Article 87 empowers Parliament to legislate on the recruitment and conditions of service for the staff of these secretariats. This provision ensures that there is a structured and consistent approach to hiring and managing the personnel who support the legislative process. Until such laws are enacted by Parliament, the article gives temporary authority to the Speaker of the National Assembly and the Chairman of the Senate, with the President's approval, to establish rules for recruitment and service conditions. This interim measure ensures that the secretariats can function smoothly even in the absence of specific legislation.

The article has not been amended since its inclusion in the original 1973 Constitution, indicating that its provisions have been deemed adequate for the functioning of the parliamentary secretariats. Its importance lies in providing a clear administrative structure for the legislative bodies, which is essential for the effective governance and legislative process in Pakistan. By delineating the roles and responsibilities regarding the secretarial staff, Article 87 helps maintain the operational integrity and independence of the Parliament.
