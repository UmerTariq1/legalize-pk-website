Article 94 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 deals with the resignation of the Prime Minister. It outlines the process by which a Prime Minister can resign and the subsequent steps that must be taken to ensure a smooth transition of power. This article is crucial as it provides a clear mechanism for political stability and continuity in governance, ensuring that there is no power vacuum when a Prime Minister steps down. According to the article, the Prime Minister can resign by submitting a written notice to the President. Upon the resignation of the Prime Minister, all Federal Ministers and Ministers of State automatically cease to hold office, which underscores the central role of the Prime Minister in the executive branch of the government.

The article also stipulates that the outgoing Prime Minister and any Federal Minister requested by the Prime Minister will continue to perform their duties until a new Prime Minister is elected and assumes office. This provision is significant as it ensures that the functions of the government continue without interruption. Furthermore, if the National Assembly is in session at the time of the resignation, it is required to immediately elect a new Prime Minister. If the Assembly is not in session, the President must summon it to meet within fourteen days to elect a new leader. This ensures a timely and orderly transition, maintaining the democratic process and preventing any potential political instability.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 94 has not been amended, indicating that the original provisions have been deemed effective and sufficient in managing the resignation and succession of the Prime Minister. The article's unaltered status suggests that it has successfully served its purpose in the constitutional framework of Pakistan, providing a reliable procedure for leadership change within the executive branch.
