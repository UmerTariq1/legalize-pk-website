Article 60 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 establishes the roles of the Chairman and Deputy Chairman of the Senate. This article is crucial as it outlines the procedure for electing these key figures within the Senate, which is the upper house of Pakistan's bicameral legislature. The Senate plays a vital role in representing the provinces and ensuring that their interests are considered in the legislative process. The Chairman and Deputy Chairman are responsible for presiding over Senate sessions, maintaining order, and ensuring that the rules of the Senate are followed. Their leadership is essential for the smooth functioning of the Senate, which in turn contributes to the stability and effectiveness of Pakistan's parliamentary system.

The original text of Article 60, as enacted in 1973, specified that the Senate must elect a Chairman and a Deputy Chairman at its first meeting and whenever these positions become vacant. Initially, the term of office for both the Chairman and Deputy Chairman was set at two years. However, this was altered by the Eighth Amendment in 1985, during the regime of General Muhammad Zia-ul-Haq. The amendment extended the term of office from two years to three years. This change likely aimed to provide greater continuity and stability in the leadership of the Senate, allowing the Chairman and Deputy Chairman more time to implement their agendas and contribute to legislative processes. The extension of the term could also reflect a broader political strategy to maintain influence and control over the Senate during a period of military rule.
