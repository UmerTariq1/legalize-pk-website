This article is concerned with the legislative lists that define the distribution of powers between the federal and provincial governments. Originally, the Constitution included a Concurrent Legislative List, which allowed both federal and provincial governments to legislate on certain matters. The Eighteenth Amendment marked a significant shift by removing references to the Concurrent Legislative List, thereby enhancing provincial autonomy. This change was part of a broader effort to decentralize power and give provinces more control over their affairs, reflecting a political move towards strengthening federalism in Pakistan.
