Article 240 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the framework for appointments and conditions of service for individuals serving in various governmental capacities within Pakistan. Its primary purpose is to delineate the authority and process by which appointments are made and service conditions are set for federal and provincial services. This article is significant because it establishes a clear division of responsibilities between the federal and provincial governments, ensuring that both levels of government have the autonomy to manage their respective services. For federal services, including those that are common to both the Federation and the Provinces, the rules are set by or under an Act of Parliament. In contrast, provincial services are governed by or under an Act of the Provincial Assembly. This structure is crucial for maintaining a balance of power and ensuring that both federal and provincial governments can effectively manage their personnel according to their specific needs and contexts.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 240 has not been amended, indicating that the original framework has been deemed adequate for managing appointments and service conditions in Pakistan. The lack of amendments suggests that the division of responsibilities between the federal and provincial governments has functioned well within the constitutional framework, providing stability and clarity in the governance of public services. This stability is important for the functioning of government institutions, as it ensures continuity and consistency in the management of human resources across different levels of government.
