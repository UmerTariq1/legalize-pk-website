Article 47 of the Constitution of Pakistan, established in 1973, outlines the procedure for the removal of the President from office. This article is significant as it provides a constitutional mechanism to ensure accountability at the highest level of the executive branch. It allows for the President to be removed on grounds of physical or mental incapacity, violation of the Constitution, or gross misconduct. The process begins with a written notice from at least half of the members of either the National Assembly or the Senate, which must be submitted to the Speaker of the National Assembly or the Chairman of the Senate. This notice must detail the specific charges or grounds for removal.

Once the notice is received, the Speaker is required to inform the President and convene a joint session of both Houses of Parliament within a specified timeframe. During this joint session, an investigation into the charges may be conducted, and the President has the right to defend themselves. The article ensures a fair process by allowing the President to be represented during the investigation and before the joint sitting. Ultimately, a resolution for removal must be passed by a two-thirds majority of the total membership of Parliament for it to take effect, at which point the President would immediately cease to hold office.

This article has not been amended since its inception, indicating that the original framers of the Constitution considered the procedure robust and comprehensive enough to address the serious matter of presidential removal. It plays a crucial role in maintaining the balance of power and upholding the rule of law by providing a clear and structured process for addressing potential misconduct or incapacity at the highest level of government.
