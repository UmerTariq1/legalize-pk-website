Article 25 of the Constitution of Pakistan, titled "Equality of citizens," is a fundamental provision that underscores the principle of equality before the law. This article establishes that all citizens are equal and entitled to equal protection under the law, which is a cornerstone of democratic governance and the rule of law. It prohibits discrimination, initially specifying that discrimination on the basis of sex alone is not permissible, and allows the state to make special provisions for the protection of women and children. This reflects a commitment to gender equality and the protection of vulnerable groups, which is essential for fostering an inclusive society.

The article was originally part of the 1973 Constitution and remained unchanged until the Eighteenth Amendment in 2010. The amendment made a subtle yet significant change by removing the word "alone" from the clause prohibiting discrimination on the basis of sex. This change broadened the scope of the prohibition, making it clear that any discrimination based on sex is not allowed, regardless of whether it is the sole factor or combined with other factors. This amendment was part of a broader effort to strengthen the constitutional framework and enhance the protection of fundamental rights in Pakistan. By eliminating any ambiguity regarding gender discrimination, the amendment reinforced the state's commitment to gender equality, aligning with international human rights standards and responding to evolving societal norms and expectations.
