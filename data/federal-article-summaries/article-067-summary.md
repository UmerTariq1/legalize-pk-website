Article 67 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, establishes the framework for the internal procedures of the legislative houses, namely the National Assembly and the Senate. This article grants each house the authority to create its own rules for conducting business and managing its proceedings. The significance of this provision lies in its empowerment of the legislative bodies to self-regulate, ensuring that they can function independently and efficiently without external interference. It also ensures continuity and legitimacy of legislative processes by stating that the proceedings remain valid even if there are vacancies or if individuals who were not entitled to participate do so. This provision is crucial for maintaining the integrity and functionality of the legislative process, allowing for uninterrupted governance.

The article also provides a transitional mechanism for the conduct of business until each house formulates its own rules. Initially, the procedures are governed by rules set by the President. This ensures that there is no procedural vacuum and that legislative business can commence smoothly from the outset. The ability to self-regulate is a fundamental aspect of parliamentary sovereignty and autonomy, which is essential for a functioning democracy. By allowing each house to determine its own procedures, the Constitution respects the principle of separation of powers and supports the legislative branch in fulfilling its role effectively.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 67 has not been amended, indicating that its original provisions have been deemed adequate and effective in facilitating the legislative process. The lack of amendments suggests that the framework established has successfully met the needs of the legislative bodies in Pakistan, providing them with the necessary flexibility and authority to manage their internal affairs. This stability reflects the enduring relevance of the article in the constitutional structure of Pakistan.
