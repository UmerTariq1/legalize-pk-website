Article 31 of the Constitution of Pakistan, titled "Islamic way of Life," was established in the original 1973 Constitution under the leadership of Prime Minister Zulfikar Ali Bhutto. This article underscores the commitment of the Pakistani state to foster an environment where Muslims can live according to Islamic principles. It emphasizes the state's role in facilitating the understanding and practice of Islam by its citizens. The article mandates the state to take steps to ensure that Muslims can order their lives in line with the fundamental principles of Islam, as derived from the Holy Quran and Sunnah.

The article outlines specific responsibilities for the state, such as making the teaching of the Holy Quran and Islamic studies compulsory in educational institutions, promoting the learning of the Arabic language, and ensuring the accurate printing and publication of the Holy Quran. Additionally, it calls for the promotion of Islamic moral standards and unity among Muslims, as well as the proper organization of religious institutions like zakat (charitable giving), auqaf (endowments), and mosques. These provisions reflect the importance of Islam in the cultural and social fabric of Pakistan and the state's role in preserving and promoting Islamic values.

Since its inception, Article 31 has not been amended, indicating a consistent approach to the integration of Islamic principles in the governance and daily life of Pakistani Muslims. The article is significant as it lays the foundation for the Islamic identity of the state and its citizens, influencing educational policies and the management of religious affairs. It highlights the constitutional commitment to uphold Islamic teachings and practices, which plays a crucial role in shaping the societal norms and values in Pakistan.
