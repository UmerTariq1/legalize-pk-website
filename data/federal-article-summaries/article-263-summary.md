Article 263 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 addresses the interpretation of gender and number within the constitutional text. Its purpose is to ensure inclusivity and clarity in the language used throughout the Constitution. Specifically, it establishes that words referring to the masculine gender should be understood to include females, and that singular terms should be interpreted to include their plural forms, and vice versa. This article is crucial because it prevents any gender-based exclusion or ambiguity in the application of constitutional provisions, ensuring that all individuals, regardless of gender, are equally considered under the law. Additionally, it provides flexibility in interpreting the number, which aids in the comprehensive application of the Constitution's provisions.

Since its inception on August 14, 1973, Article 263 has not been amended. This indicates that the original framers of the Constitution foresaw the importance of inclusive language and the need for clarity in legal interpretation, which has remained relevant and uncontested over the years. The lack of amendments suggests a consensus on the necessity of these interpretative rules, reflecting a foundational commitment to gender equality and linguistic precision in Pakistan's constitutional framework. This article plays a subtle yet significant role in ensuring that the Constitution is applied fairly and inclusively, without the need for constant revisions to accommodate changes in societal norms regarding gender and number.
