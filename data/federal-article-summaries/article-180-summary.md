Article 180 of the Constitution of Pakistan deals with the appointment of an Acting Chief Justice of Pakistan. This article is crucial as it ensures the continuity of the judiciary's leadership in situations where the Chief Justice is unable to perform their duties due to absence, vacancy, or other reasons. The President of Pakistan is given the authority to appoint an acting Chief Justice from among the judges of the Supreme Court, ensuring that the highest court in the country remains functional and that there is no disruption in the administration of justice.

The article has undergone two significant amendments since its inception. The first change came with the Fifth Amendment in 1976, which altered the criteria for appointing an Acting Chief Justice. Originally, the most senior judge was automatically appointed to act as Chief Justice. However, the amendment allowed the President to appoint any judge of the Supreme Court who had not previously held the position of Chief Justice, except under this specific article. This change provided the President with more flexibility in selecting an acting Chief Justice, potentially allowing for considerations beyond mere seniority, such as expertise or temperament.

The second amendment, the Twenty-seventh Amendment in 2025, made a terminological change by replacing "Chief Justice of Pakistan" with "Chief Justice of the Supreme Court." This change was likely made for consistency and clarity, aligning the terminology with other parts of the Constitution or legal documents. While this amendment did not alter the substance of the article, it reflects an ongoing effort to maintain clarity and precision in legal language. These amendments collectively illustrate the evolving nature of constitutional provisions to address practical governance needs and ensure the judiciary's effective functioning.
