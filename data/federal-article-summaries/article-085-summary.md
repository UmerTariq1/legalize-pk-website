Article 85 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the concept of "votes on account." This article is significant as it provides the National Assembly with the authority to grant funds in advance for a portion of the financial year, specifically not exceeding four months. This mechanism is crucial for ensuring the continuity of government operations and financial stability, especially when the formal budgetary process is not yet complete. The article allows the government to manage its expenses temporarily while the detailed budgetary procedures, as outlined in Articles 82 and 83, are being finalized. This provision ensures that essential government functions can continue without interruption, even if the formal budget has not been fully approved.

The article has remained unchanged since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, indicating its foundational role in the financial governance of Pakistan. By allowing for temporary financial measures, Article 85 plays a vital role in maintaining the smooth operation of government services and avoiding potential disruptions that could arise from delays in the budget approval process. This stability is essential for both the government and the citizens, as it ensures that public services and government obligations are met without interruption.
