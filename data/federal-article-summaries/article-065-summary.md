Article 65 of the Constitution of Pakistan 1973 establishes the requirement for members elected to either House of Parliament to take an oath before they can officially sit or vote in the legislative proceedings. This article is crucial as it ensures that elected representatives formally commit to their duties and responsibilities as outlined in the Constitution. The oath serves as a solemn promise to uphold the Constitution and perform their roles with integrity, which is fundamental to maintaining the rule of law and the democratic process in Pakistan.

Since its inception in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 65 has not been amended. This consistency underscores the enduring importance of the oath-taking process as a foundational element of parliamentary procedure. By requiring members to take an oath, the article reinforces accountability and the ethical conduct expected of public officials. It also symbolizes the transition from being elected to actively participating in the governance of the country, ensuring that all members are aligned with the constitutional values and principles before they engage in legislative activities.
