Article 225 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the resolution of election disputes. It establishes that any challenge to the validity of an election to either the National Assembly or a Provincial Assembly can only be made through an election petition. This petition must be presented to a tribunal and follow procedures specified by an Act of Parliament. The purpose of this article is to provide a clear and structured mechanism for addressing grievances related to elections, ensuring that disputes are handled in a formal and legal manner rather than through public or political agitation.

This article is significant because it underscores the importance of a fair and transparent electoral process, which is a cornerstone of democratic governance. By stipulating that election disputes must be resolved through a legal framework, it helps maintain the integrity of the electoral process and ensures that any allegations of malpractice are thoroughly investigated and adjudicated by a competent authority. This not only protects the rights of candidates and voters but also upholds public confidence in the democratic system.

Since its inclusion in the original 1973 Constitution, Article 225 has not been amended. This suggests that the mechanism it establishes for handling election disputes has been considered adequate and effective over the years. Its unaltered state reflects a consensus on the importance of having a dedicated legal process for election-related issues, which is crucial for the stability and legitimacy of Pakistan's democratic institutions.
