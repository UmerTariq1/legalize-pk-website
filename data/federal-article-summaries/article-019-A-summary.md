Article 19-A of the Constitution of Pakistan, introduced through the Eighteenth Amendment in 2010, establishes the right to information for every citizen. This article is significant as it empowers citizens by granting them access to information on matters of public importance. The right to information is a fundamental aspect of a transparent and accountable government, enabling citizens to make informed decisions and participate more actively in democratic processes. By allowing access to governmental information, this article aims to reduce corruption and increase government accountability, fostering a more open and participatory society.

The introduction of Article 19-A was part of a broader effort to enhance democratic rights and freedoms in Pakistan. The Eighteenth Amendment, enacted during the presidency of Asif Ali Zardari, was a comprehensive reform package that aimed to decentralize power and strengthen parliamentary democracy. By including the right to information, the amendment aligned Pakistan with global trends towards greater transparency and openness in governance. The practical effect of this change is that citizens can now request information from public bodies, subject to certain regulations and reasonable restrictions, which can lead to greater scrutiny of government actions and policies. This empowerment of citizens is crucial for fostering a culture of accountability and ensuring that public officials are answerable to the people they serve.
