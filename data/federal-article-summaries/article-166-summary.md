Article 166 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, establishes the authority of the federal government to engage in borrowing and to provide guarantees, using the Federal Consolidated Fund as security. This article is crucial as it outlines the financial powers of the federal government, enabling it to manage the country's finances by borrowing funds when necessary. The ability to borrow is essential for managing the national budget, funding development projects, and addressing fiscal deficits. The article ensures that such borrowing is conducted within limits set by the Parliament, thereby providing a check on the executive's financial powers and ensuring accountability and transparency in financial matters.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 166 has not been amended, indicating that the original framework for federal borrowing and guarantees has remained consistent over the years. This stability suggests that the mechanisms for financial management and oversight established by this article have been deemed adequate by successive governments. The lack of amendments also reflects a consensus on the importance of maintaining a balance between granting the federal government the flexibility to manage finances and ensuring parliamentary oversight to prevent misuse of borrowing powers. This article plays a vital role in maintaining the financial integrity and stability of the federal government, impacting how public funds are managed and utilized for the country's development.
