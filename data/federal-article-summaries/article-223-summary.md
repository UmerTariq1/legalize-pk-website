Article 223 of the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973, addresses the issue of double membership in legislative bodies. Its primary purpose is to prevent any individual from holding multiple legislative seats simultaneously, which could lead to conflicts of interest and inefficiencies in governance. The article specifies that a person cannot be a member of both the National Assembly and the Senate, a member of a provincial assembly and a national legislative body, or hold seats in more than one provincial assembly. Additionally, it restricts holding more than one seat within the same legislative body.

The article does, however, allow individuals to run for multiple seats simultaneously. If elected to more than one, the individual must resign from all but one seat within thirty days of the election results. Failure to do so results in the automatic vacation of all seats except the one to which they were last elected. This provision ensures that while candidates can explore multiple opportunities, they must ultimately commit to serving in a single capacity, thereby maintaining the integrity and focus of legislative responsibilities.

Since its inception in 1973, Article 223 has not been amended, indicating a consistent approach to managing potential conflicts of interest in legislative representation. This stability underscores the importance of clear and unambiguous rules in maintaining the orderly function of Pakistan's parliamentary system, ensuring that elected officials are dedicated to their roles without the distraction or divided loyalties that could arise from holding multiple positions.
